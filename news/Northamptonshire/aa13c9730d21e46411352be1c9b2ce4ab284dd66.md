Police officers are appealing for witnesses after a woman was assaulted during an attempted robbery in Kettering Road, Northampton.

The incident took place between 6.30pm and 6.50pm on Sunday, November 29, as the woman in her 30s walked with a male friend along Kettering Road, near to the Racecourse.

As the pair walked past a shop, a female offender pushed the woman, causing her to fall backwards into a car and assaulted her. The woman was then chased onto the Racecourse, where she was further assaulted.

Her friend attempted to intervene, however was threatened with a knife by a male, who was with the offender. The woman, who is in the early stages of pregnancy, required hospital treatment.

The female offender was white, 5ft 4in and of a medium build with a red short bob hairstyle. She was wearing a black waist-length coat, grey leggings with coloured stripes around the ankle and black Nike Air Max trainers.

The male offender was white, 5ft 1in and of a slim build with blond hair which was shaved at the sides and worn in a man bun with stubble. He was wearing a blue tracksuit top, grey T-shirt and blue tracksuit bottoms.

Anyone with information or anyone who may have witnessed this incident is asked to call Northamptonshire Police on 101 quoting reference number 20000632145. Or alternatively, if you wish to stay anonymous, call Crimestoppers on 0800 555111.
