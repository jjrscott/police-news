January 29, 2021

Share by email

Police arrested three suspects within minutes of receiving a report of three males causing damage to a property and making threats with knives.

Officers were called to an address in Clare Road, Sutton-in-Ashfield, shortly after 7.30pm on Wednesday (27 January 2021).

It was reported that a window had been smashed and the suspects had made verbal threats, claiming to be armed with knives, before making off. No-one was hurt during the incident.

Police swiftly arrived and arrested three suspects nearby. A knife was also recovered.

An 18-year-old man and 15-year-old boy were detained on suspicion of affray, criminal damage and possessing an offensive weapon in a public place.

Another 18-year-old man was arrested on suspicion of affray, criminal damage, aiding the possession an offensive weapon in a public place and being in possession of a Class C drug.

They were all bailed as officers continue with their enquiries.

Detective Inspector Nick Hall, of Nottinghamshire Police, said: "Our work is ongoing to establish the full circumstances of this incident.

"I'd like to reassure people that Nottinghamshire Police takes all reports of incidents involving knives extremely seriously, we will respond quickly and we will investigate all crimes reported to us."
