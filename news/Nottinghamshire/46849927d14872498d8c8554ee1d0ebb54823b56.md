An incident which saw a police officer spat at has led to a man being charged in connection with the incident.

Officers from Nottinghamshire Police were called at around 4.45pm in 22 December to a report that a man threatened with a member of public.

Police attended a house in Stockwell Gate in Mansfield and arrested a man on suspicion of assault. While being arrested, the man is reported to have spat in an officer’s face and then allegedly thrown punches at two more officers. He was further arrested on suspicion of assault of an emergency worker.

Daniel Groves, 24, of Willbowbridge Lane in Sutton-in-Ashfield has been charged for common assault of an emergency workers and will appear at Nottingham Magistrates Court on the 8 February 2021.

Inspector Sue Wain of Nottinghamshire Police, said: “Emergency workers are selflessly putting themselves in harm’s way to continue protecting the public during the Coronavirus pandemic.

“To think that anyone would deliberately spit at an officer at this time, causing anxiety to them and their family, is despicable.”
