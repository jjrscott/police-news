Detectives investigating after a 17-year-old boy suffered stab wounds to his stomach and forearm have released images of three people who might be able to help.

Officers from Nottinghamshire Police were called to the Hardstaff Road area of Sneinton Dale after the incident which happpened between 9.30pm and 10pm on Thursday 17 December.

The victim was treated for his injuries at the Queen's Medical Centre and has since been released after spending several days in hospital with a serious stomach wound.

An 18-year-old man and a 19-year-old man have been arrested in connection with the incident and have both been released on police bail while enquiries continue.

Detective Constable Gavin Harper, said: "We are keen to speak to these people who we believe were in the area around the time the incident took place.

"Luckily the victim's injuries are not life-threatening but it is clearly distressing for his family and for people who live in this area.

"My team have been working to establish who was behind the attack and we are determined to bring them to justice."

If you recognise the men in the images, please call us on 101 quoting incident 701 of 17 December. You can also contact Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111.
