A man has been arrested after three people were injured in a high-speed road traffic collision.

The incident took shortly after 12.30am this morning, when two cars collided at the junction of Radford Boulevard and Hartley Road, Nottingham.

The lone occupant of one of the cars was cut free from the wreckage by firefighters and taken to hospital with serious injuries. He is said to be in a stable condition.

The driver and passenger of the other vehicle were also taken to hospital for treatment.

A 26-year-old man has been arrested on suspicion of dangerous driving.

Localised road closures will remain in place throughout the morning including at Radford Boulevard with its junctions with Hartley Road. Motorists are advised to avoid the area as investigations continue.

Inspector Paul Hennessy, of Nottinghamshire Police, said: “This was a serious collision that has left two people with injuries. Officers will remain at the scene for some time as detailed accident investigation work is carried out. Anyone with any additional information about this incident is asked to call 101 quoting incident 12 of 13 December.”
