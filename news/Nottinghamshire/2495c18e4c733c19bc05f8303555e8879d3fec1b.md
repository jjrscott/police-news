December 31, 2020

Share by email

A pregnant woman was hit with a paving slab after the windows of two cars were smashed in unprovoked attacks.

Officers were called to Stoneyford Road, Stanton Hill, shortly after 9.30pm yesterdat when the vehicles were attacked.

The first incident occurred in the car park of a takeaway restaurant, where a man smashed the rear windscreen and passenger-side window of a car.

The second occurred just moments later when a paving slab was thrown through the passenger-side widow of a vehicle waiting at the junction of Brand Lane.

The slab hit a pregnant woman in the chest and she was taken to hospital for precautionary checks.

A 24-year-old man was arrested on suspicion of criminal damage and assault and taken to hospital for treatment. He is now in police custody.

Inspector Mark Dickson, District Commander for Ashfield, said: “This was a distressing incident that understandably caused a great deal of distress to the victims.

“Officers were on the scene very quickly and I would like to commend them for the calm and professional way in which they handled a very challenging situation.”

If you have any additional information about this incident please call 101 quoting incident 640 of December 30.
