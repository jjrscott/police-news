Warwickshire Police is appealing for information following the theft of bike from park in Nuneaton.

Officers were called at 5.52pm on Thursday (18 March) to a report an 11-year-old boy had had his bike stolen at Riversley Park in Avenue Road. The theft occurred near to Pingles Leisure Centre.

The bike is similar to the one pictured below but has blue handle grips that are slightly worn.

The offender is describes as being a white male, aged around 16-17 years old and around 5ft 2in tall. He was of medium build, had black hair and was wearing a black coat, grey hoody, black joggers and a brownish baseball cap. The offender was accompanied by two other males who were also riding bikes.

Anyone who witnessed the incident, can help identify the offender or help locate the bike is asked to contact Warwickshire Police on 101 quoting incident 286 of 18 March 2021 or the independent charity Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111 or via their online form at: https://crimestoppers-uk.org/give-information/give-information-online/
