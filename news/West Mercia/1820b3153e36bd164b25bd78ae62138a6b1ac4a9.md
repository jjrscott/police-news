Four men who carried out a burglary and vehicle thefts have been jailed.

_(Pictured above: Top left to right - Lee Cooper, Oliver Cooper; Bottom left to right - Shaun Cooper, Ben Wilson)_

They pleaded guilty to a number offences at Worcester Crown Court on Monday 7 October and were sentenced to a total of more than 10 years in prison.

The offences relate to a car key burglary and vehicle thefts that took place in Worcester on 28 September 2018.

Oliver Cooper, 19, of young offender institution at HMP Featherstone pleaded guilty to one count of burglary of a dwelling and theft of a motor vehicle. He was sentenced to 30 months in prison.

Lee Cooper, 18, of Werrington Young Offender Institution pleaded guilty to one count of burglary of a dwelling and theft of a motor vehicle. He was sentenced to 30 months in prison.

Shaun Cooper, 20, of Sandon Road, Birmingham, pleaded guilty to one count of burglary of a dwelling and theft of a motor vehicle. He was sentenced to 30 months in prison.

Ben Wilson, 24, of HMP Hewell pleaded guilty to one count of burglary of a dwelling and theft of a motor vehicle. He was sentenced to 36 months in prison. Wilson also admitted a further charge of handling stolen goods which he will serve concurrently.

Detective Sergeant Scott Chivers from West Mercia Police said: "We welcome the convictions of the four individuals; burglary and car theft can be a very emotive and intrusive crime and I hope the victims are reassured that justice has been done.

"Tackling burglary remains a priority for West Mercia Police and we are committed to protecting our local communities."

Anyone who is a victim of burglary or vehicle theft that feels they may need support, or may not want to contact the police can contact West Mercia Police's Victim Advice Line which is a free, independent and confidential support service. People can call 0800 952 3000 or visit www.victimadviceline.org.uk
