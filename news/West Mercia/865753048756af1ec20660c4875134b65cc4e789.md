Updated News Post

Following a collision between a car and mobility scooter at Crossley Retail Park, Kidderminster on Sunday (5 January), we are sad to announce the man who was flown to the Queen Elizabeth Birmingham has died due to his injuries.

Clement Dalley, from Kidderminster, died yesterday (Monday) at the QE hospital. The 78-year-old was well known locally for being the owner of family business, Dalley Seeds, a seed merchant in the town.

Police were called to reports of a collision between a vehicle and a mobility scooter near Crossley Retail Park, Kidderminster.

A blue Nissan Juke and a mobility scooter collided on the carriageway near to the A456.

The roads around the junction were closed from around 1pm to 5pm.

PC Scott Gee said: "Our thoughts are with Mr Dalley's family at this time and we are keen to speak with anyone who may have seen the incident, which occurred on the ring road near to the Crossley Retail Park, at around 1pm on Sunday involving a Nissan Juke and a mobility scooter so that we can get a better understanding of what has occurred.

"We are especially keen to see any dash cam footage from the area at the time of the incident. If you have any information please call 101."

If you have any information regarding the accident or dash cam footage please contact West Mercia Police on 101 quoting 300s of 5 January.
