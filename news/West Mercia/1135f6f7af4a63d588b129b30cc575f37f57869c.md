A man has been arrested in connection with an incident in Hadley Road in Telford this morning.

At approximately 6.15am this morning police were called to Hadley Road over concerns a woman was being threatened by a man with a knife.

The woman was taken to hospital with injuries consistent with knife wounds. Her injuries are not believed to be life-threatening.

Throughout the morning police have been carrying out a search of the local area.

A 60 year old man has been arrested on suspicion of attempted murder and is currently in police custody.

Increased patrols will remain throughout the day to offer reassurance to the local community.

Telford policing commander Superintendent Paul Moxley said: "We can confirm a man has now been arrested and is in police custody. We understand this incident caused some disruption this morning, in particular during rush hour and would like to thank the local community for the patience and understanding as we dealt with the incident."

Any witnesses, including any motorists with dash cam, are asked to contact West Mercia Police Police on 101 quoting reference 70s 141019 or alternatively information can be passed to Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111.

Notes to editors

We understand there has been some indication schools were on lockdown however we do not understand that to be the case and at no point was there any intelligence to suggest we would need to give any advice to this affect.
