A man has been arrested and cannabis plants have been seized by police in Pinvin, near Pershore.

After concerns were raised by a member of the public, officers visited an empty business in the village and located a large quantity of cannabis plants.

Officers visited the address on Thursday 25 February and discovered the plants growing. A 28 year-old man was arrested on suspicion of production of cannabis.

Inspector Alex warner said: "We'd like to thank the member of the public who contacted us and raised their concerns about the possibility of drugs being cultivated in the local area. We want to continue to make our communities even safer and we always appreciate the public sharing information with us."

If you are aware or think you know of any criminality such as this, or if you think someone is growing or selling drugs please contact your Safer Neighbourhood Team. If you have information but don't feel comfortable speaking to police, you can speak to the independent charity Crimestoppers. It is 100% anonymous, they never ask your name and they cannot trace your call or I.P address. You can contact them online or by calling 0800 555 111.
