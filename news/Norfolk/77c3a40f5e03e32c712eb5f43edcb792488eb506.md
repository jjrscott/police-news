Police can confirm a woman who suffered serious injuries after being involved in a collision with a bus in Norwich has died.

The collision happened shortly before 2.10pm on Red Lion Street on Monday 14 December, near to the junction with Rampant Horse Street, involving a double decker bus and a pedestrian.

The pedestrian, a woman in her 80s, suffered multiple serious injuries and was taken to the Norfolk and Norwich University Hospital where she sadly died as a result of her injuries on Monday 21 December.

Officers would still like to hear from anyone who may have witnessed the collision and didn’t speak to police at the scene. Witnesses should contact PC David Reed at the Roads and Armed Policing Team at Wymondham on 101.

## 15/12/2020 | 08:50

Police are appealing witnesses following a collision involving a bus and a pedestrian in Norwich yesterday afternoon (Monday 14 December 2020).

The collision happened shortly before 2.10pm on Red Lion Street, near to the junction with Rampant Horse Street, involving a double decker bus and a pedestrian.

The pedestrian, a woman in her 80s, suffered multiple serious injuries and was taken to the Norfolk and Norwich University Hospital for treatment.

The road was closed while emergency services attended the scene and reopened just before 4.30pm.

Officers are keen to hear from anyone who may have witnessed the collision and didn’t speak to police at the scene.

Witnesses should contact PC David Reed at the Roads and Armed Policing Team at Wymondham on 101.
