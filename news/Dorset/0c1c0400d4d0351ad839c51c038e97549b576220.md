# Witness appeal following altercation in Wareham

23 August 2018

* * *

Detectives investigating an altercation in Wareham are appealing for witnesses or anyone with information to come forward.

At around 9.20pm yesterday, Wednesday 22 August 2018, Dorset Police received reports of a disturbance in the area of Mill Lane.

Officers attended and found a 15-year-old local boy receiving medical treatment for a small puncture wound to his leg at Abbot's Quay. He was subsequently taken to hospital for further treatment.

Another 15-year-old local boy suffered facial injuries and was taken to hospital for treatment.

A 15-year-old boy from Wareham has been arrested on suspicion of inflicting grievous bodily harm and is assisting officers with their enquiries.

Detective Sergeant Tom Hall, of Weymouth CID, said: "We are carrying out a number of enquiries into what happened in Wareham town centre last night, including a trawl of CCTV footage and house to house.

"I am appealing to anyone who witnessed the disturbance or has any information as to how the boys came to sustain their injuries to contact police."

Neighbourhood Inspector Andy Keel, of Purbeck police, said: "I understand this incident will cause some concern among the local community in Wareham but I would like to reassure residents that a detailed investigation is underway into what happened.

"Local officers will also be carrying out high visibility patrols in the town and can be approached with any concerns."

Anyone with information is asked to contact Dorset Police at www.dorset.police.uk, via email 101@dorset.pnn.police.uk or by calling 101, quoting occurrence number 55180136094. Alternatively, contact Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111 or via www.crimestoppers-uk.org.
