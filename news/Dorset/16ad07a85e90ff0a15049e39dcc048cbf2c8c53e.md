# Appeal after man threatened in Weymouth

06 June 2017

* * *

Officers are appealing for witnesses after a man reported being threatened and chased by another man carrying a broken bottle in Weymouth.

Police received a report at around 6.20am today, Tuesday 6 June 2017, relating to an altercation in Ranelagh Road, near to Heron Court.

The offender was seen shouting at a woman and when the victim tried to intervene, he became aggressive and pulled a broken bottle from his pocket.

He then chased the victim, a local man in his 30s, along Ranelagh Road toward Cassiobury Road.

The offender is described as white, aged around 30 and approximately five feet ten inches tall. He wore a black beanie hat, white T-shirt with blue or green writing on it, black jogging bottoms and blue or black Nike trainers.

Police Sergeant Steve Hughes, of Weymouth police, said: “I am keen to identify the woman who the man was shouting at in Ranelagh Road.

“I would also appeal to anyone who may have witnessed the incident or who may be able to identify the offender to please contact police.”

Anyone with information is asked to contact Dorset Police at www.dorset.police.uk, via email 101@dorset.pnn.police.uk or by calling 101, quoting incident number 6:36. Alternatively, contact Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555111 or via www.crimestoppers-uk.org.
