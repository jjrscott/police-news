# Fail-to-stop injury collision in Bournemouth â€“ witness appeal

03 May 2017

* * *

Officers investigating a reported fail-to-stop collision in Bournemouth are appealing for witnesses to come forward.

At approximately 9.30pm on Friday 28 April 2017 a road traffic collision occurred between a bicycle and a car on Gladdis Road in the West Howe area of the town. It was reported that the vehicle failed to stop at the scene.

The rider of the bicycle - a 57-year-old man from Bournemouth - sustained a broken ankle.

Police Constable Mike Henstridge, of the traffic unit, said: "I would ask anyone who witnessed the incident to please contact me."

Anyone with information is asked to contact Dorset Police at www.dorset.police.uk, via email 101@dorset.pnn.police.uk or by calling 101, quoting occurrence number 55170062217. Alternatively, contact Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555111 or via www.crimestoppers-uk.org.
