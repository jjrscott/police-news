# Appeal after road rage incident in Dorchester

24 April 2017

* * *

Officers are appealing for information after a road rage incident involving a motorist and two cyclists in Dorchester.

The incident happened at around 2.30pm on Friday 7 April 2017 in High West Street.

Two cyclists, wearing lycra and helmets, were involved in an altercation with a motorist driving a grey Audi A4.

It was reported that the motorist attempted to knock the cyclists off their bikes with his vehicle, causing one of them to hit another vehicle in the opposite lane.

Officers are keen to speak to the cyclists involved in the incident or anyone who witnessed it.

Police Constable Dermot Shanahan, of Dorchester police, said: "I am keen to speak to the two cyclists involved in this incident to get their accounts.

"I would also ask anyone who witnessed the incident who has not already spoken to police to please contact me."

Anyone with information is asked to contact Dorset Police at www.dorset.police.uk, via email 101@dorset.pnn.police.uk or by calling 101, quoting occurrence number 55170050806. Alternatively contact Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 55511 or via www.crimestoppers-uk.org.
