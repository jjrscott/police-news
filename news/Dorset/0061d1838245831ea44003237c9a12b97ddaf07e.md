# Appeal for witnesses after man stabbed in Bournemouth

05 December 2017

* * *

Detectives are appealing for witnesses after a man was stabbed in Bournemouth.

The incident happened at around 8pm on Monday 4 December 2017 at the junction of Lydford Road and Lydford Gardens.

The victim - a Bournemouth man aged in his 20s - sustained a stab wound to his arm that was inflicted with an unknown weapon. He attended hospital and was released after treatment.

A search for the offenders was carried out, including support from NPAS helicopter, however no one was identified.

The suspects are described as two white men. The first is believed to be around six feet tall with dark hair and the second suspect is thought to be about five feet seven inches tall and had his hood up.

Detective Sergeant Garry Knight, of Bournemouth CID, said: "We continue to investigate this incident and are looking into why this victim was targeted.

"I am appealing for any witnesses, or anyone who saw any suspicious activity in the area around the time of the incident, to please contact police."

Anyone with information is asked to contact Dorset Police at www.dorset.police.uk, via email 101@dorset.pnn.police.uk or by calling 101, quoting occurrence number 55170188557. Alternatively contact Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555111 or via www.crimestoppers-uk.org.
