Posted on 05:42:00 on 19th May 2019

Police were called at 3:36am this morning (19th May), to the report of a road traffic collision on the A66 Stainburn Bypass at Great Clifton. The collision involved a Citroen and a pedestrian.

The pedestrian, a 37-year old man from Cockermouth, suffered a serious injuries, he has been taken to Cumberland Infirmary Carlisle in a Critical condition.

The driver of the Citroen, a 25-year-old man from Cockermouth, was uninjured.

The Stainburn Bypass is closed in both directions and will remain closed for the foreseeable future. Diversions are in place and congestion is expected in this area. Drivers are asked to plan their journey accordingly.

Visit the traffic link map at www.cumbria.police.uk/traffic-link-map to keep up to date with the latest situation on the roads.

Police are appealing for any witnesses to contact PC 2344 Ross on 101, or call CrimeStoppers, anonymously, on 0800 555 111. Police would like to speak to the driver of a white taxi, as a potential witness, who was seen in the area at the time of the incident.
