* ### A man was approached by a woman who made a racist remark in Market Place, Hertford.

 * ### It happened when there is likely to have been other people around who may have heard what the woman said.

 * ### Anyone who witnessed the incident is asked to contact police.

Officers investigating a racially aggravated incident in Hertford are appealing for information and witnesses.

Between 11.05am and 11.15am on Friday, January 26, the victim - a man aged in his 30s - was in Market Place when he was approached by the offender - a woman - who made a racist remark before walking off.

PC James Wingate said: "This happened at a time when there is likely to have been many people in the town centre who may have heard the remark that was made to the victim.

"I'm appealing for anyone who witnessed the incident to get in touch as you may have information that could help our enquiries."

Anyone with information is asked to contact PC Wingate by emailing james.wingate2@herts.pnn.police.uk, calling the non-emergency number 101, quoting crime reference A1/18/297 or report information online at www.herts.police.uk/report.

Alternatively, you can contact the independent crime-fighting charity Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111 or through their Anonymous Online Form at www.crimestoppers-uk.org. No personal details are taken, information cannot be traced or recorded and you will never need to go to court.
