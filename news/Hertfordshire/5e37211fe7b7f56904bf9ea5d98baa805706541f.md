* ### A wallet and an iPhone were stolen from a man as he shopped in Watford.

 * ### The victim's bank cards were later used at a supermarket.

 * ### Police would like to speak with the man pictured as he may be able to assist with the investigation.

Police investigating a theft in Watford have released images of a man they would like to speak to for help with their enquiries.

Between 10.45am and 11.15am on Saturday, June 9 a man was shopping in Marks and Spencer at the Intu centre, with his wife, when he noticed his wallet and iPhone were missing.

While cancelling his bank cards, the victim was informed they had since been used to make transactions at a supermarket.

Officers would like to speak with the person pictured as he may have information which could assist their investigation.

Anyone with information is asked to contact PC Ryan Doyle the non-emergency number 101, quoting crime reference 41/5963/18. You can also report information online at https://www.herts.police.uk/Report.

Alternatively, you can contact the independent crime-fighting charity Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111 or through their Anonymous Online Form at www.crimestoppers-uk.org. No personal details are taken, information cannot be traced or recorded and you will never need to go to court.

Can you help us to identify this man?
