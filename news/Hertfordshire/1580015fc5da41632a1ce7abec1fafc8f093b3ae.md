 * **It happened between 4.30pm and 5.19pm when a window was smashed on the Ford Transit and the dog was taken**
 * **Any witnesses or anyone with information is asked to contact Hertfordshire Constabulary on the non-emergency number 101, quoting reference 41/7467/19**

Officers investigating a theft of a dog in St Albans are appealing for witnesses or anyone with information to get in contact.

Between 4.30pm and 5.19pm on Wednesday, January 23, a French bulldog named Roxy was stolen from her owner's white Ford Transit that was parked at the Noke Thistle Hotel on Watford Road.

The victim returned to his car to find a window had been smashed and his dog had been taken.

Roxy was wearing a blue collar with a bow tie on it.

Stolen French bulldog Roxy

Any witnesses or anyone with information should contact Sergeant Paul Caro on _paul.caro@herts.pnn.police.uk_ or the non-emergency number 101, quoting reference 41/7467/19. You can also report information online at www.herts.police.uk/Report

Alternatively, you can stay 100% anonymous by contacting the independent charity Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111 or via their untraceable online form at www.crimestoppers-uk.org.
