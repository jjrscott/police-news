* ### Witnesses are being sought to an affray in Stevenage.

 * ### lt took place around 8.30pm on Wednesday 1 July in the Argyle Way area.

 * ### Call 101 quoting reference 41/51508/20 if you can help.

We are appealing for witnesses and information following an incident of affray in Stevenage.

At around 8.30pm on Wednesday 1 July police were called to reports of three men fighting in an underpass from Argyle Way to Fairview Road and then onto Argyle Way towards the Leisure Park. It was reported a baseball bat was used during this.

Detective Constable Jodie Bolton, who is investigating, said: "A man has been arrested in relation to this incident and we would like to trace the other two men involved. If this was you we'd like you to come forward to assist the investigation.

"Additionally, we are appealing for any witnesses to it or anyone who has further information about this who has not already been in touch with us to make contact to assist our enquiries.

"If you are able to help I can be contacted directly via email at jodie.bolton2@herts.pnn.police.uk"

You can report information online at herts.police.uk/report, speak to an operator in our Force Communications Room via online web chat at herts.police.uk/contact or call our non-emergency number 101 quoting reference 41/51508/20.

If a crime is in progress or someone's life is in danger, call 999 immediately.

Alternatively you can call Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111 or report information online at crimestoppers-uk.org.

* A 21 year old man from Stevenage was arrested on suspicion of affray, he has been released under investigation while enquiries continue.
