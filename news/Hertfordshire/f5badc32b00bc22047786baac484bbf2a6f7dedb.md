Officers investigating a racially aggravated public order offence in St Albans are appealing for information and witnesses.

Between around 2.30pm to 5pm on Tuesday 24 July criminal damage was caused to playground equipment and a storage unit at Charles Morris Hall in Tyttenhanger Green.

Offensive graffiti had been spray painted onto both the playground equipment and the storage unit.

Sergeant Mark Williams, from the local Safer Neighbourhood Team, said: "Behaviour of this kind will not be tolerated by the police.

"I am appealing for anyone who might have seen what happened or anyone who has information about the incident to get in touch.

"If you can help with our investigation please call 101 or email me at mark.williams2@herts.pnn.police.uk quoting crime reference 41/21040/18. You can also report information online at https://www.herts.police.uk/Report.

"Alternatively, you can contact the independent crime-fighting charity Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111 or through their Anonymous Online Form at www.crimestoppers-uk.org. No personal details are taken, information cannot be traced or recorded and you will never need to go to court."
