* ### A shop barcode scanning device was stolen from the BP Service Station in St Albans.

 * ### Officers would like to speak to the man pictured as he may have information that could help the investigation.

 * ### Anyone who recognises him is asked to contact PC Barrie Delaney.

Officers investigating a theft in St Albans are releasing a CCTV image of a man they would like to speak to as part of their enquiries.

At around 8.45pm on Monday, July 30, a man entered the BP Service Station in Verulam Road and stole a hand held scanning device, which is used to read barcodes.

Police would like to identify the man pictured as they believe he has information that could help the investigation.

Anyone who recognises him is asked to contact PC Barrie Delaney by emailing barrie.delaney2@herts.pnn.police.uk, calling Hertfordshire Constabulary's non-emergency number 101, quoting crime reference 41/22742/18, or report information online at www.herts.police.uk/Report.

Alternatively contact the independent crime-fighting charity Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111 or through their anonymous online form at www.crimestoppers-uk.org. No personal details are taken, information cannot be traced or recorded and you will never need to go to court.
