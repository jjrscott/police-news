A man has been charged with possession of Class A and B drugs and possession of a knife in St Albans.

At around 4.30pm on Tuesday, October 9, officers stopped a grey Audi S3 in Elm Drive, St Albans.

A search of the vehicle was carried out and drugs and a knife were found inside.

Syed Shah, aged 22 of College Lane, Hatfield, has been charged with possession of Class A and B drugs (cocaine and cannabis) and possession of a knife. He will appear at St Albans Magistrates Court on Friday, November 9.

A 21 year old man from Middlesex, a 20 year old man from Luton and a 22 year old man from Hatfield, were also arrested on suspicion of possession with intent to supply cannabis. The 21 year old man from Middlesex and the 20 year old man from Luton were also arrested on suspicion of possession of an offensive weapon. They have been released with no further police action.

If you are worried about drug or knife crime in your area, you can report information via the non-emergency number 101 or report information online at https://www.herts.police.uk/Report.

Alternatively, you can contact the independent crime-fighting charity Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111 or through their Anonymous Online Form at www.crimestoppers-uk.org. No personal details are taken, information cannot be traced or recorded and you will never need to go to court.

If a crime is in progress or someone's life is in danger, you should always call 999.
