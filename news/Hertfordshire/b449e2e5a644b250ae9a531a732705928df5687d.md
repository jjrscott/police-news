 * ### Officers investigating a burglary in Watford are releasing images of watches that were stolen in a bid to trace them

 * ### The burglary happened on Sutton Road between 5.45pm on Monday, October 2 and 12.15am on Tuesday, October 3

 * ### Anyone with information should contact DC Judith Muskett on 101, quoting crime reference C1/17/5479

Officers investigating a burglary in Watford are releasing images of watches that were stolen in a bid to trace them.

Between 5.45pm on Monday, October 2 and 12.15am on Tuesday, October 3, offender(s) gained entry to a property in Sutton Road and carried out an untidy search.

Along with a quantity of cash, the following items were taken:

 * An Armani watch (pictured)
 * A Rado watch (pictured)
 * A Gucci watch (pictured)
 * A brass Atlantean pendulum
 * A small black velvet bag with satin lining
 * A diamond gold chain necklace
 * A gold diamond wedding ring
 * A gold chain necklace
 * Two white gold diamond rings
 * A silver bracelet
 * A pair of diamond earrings
 * A Luis Vuitton handbag
 * A Luis Vuitton purse
 * An Armani watch
 * Two gold rings

Detective Constable Judith Muskett, who is investigating, said: "These items are of great sentimental value and I would like to hear from anyone who thinks they may have come across the items or seen them advertised for sale. Similarly, if you witnessed any suspicious activity in the area around the times stated, please get in touch. Any information, no matter how seemingly insignificant, could greatly assist our investigation."

Anyone with information is asked to contact DC Judith Muskett via the Hertfordshire Constabulary non-emergency number 101, quoting crime reference C1/17/5479 or report information online.

Alternatively, you can contact the independent crime-fighting charity Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111 or through their Anonymous Online Form. No personal details are taken, information cannot be traced or recorded and you will never need to go to court.
