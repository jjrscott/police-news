* ### The theft occurred in the Gossamers on April 8.

 * ### A money fob was stolen and subsequently used to purchase £100 worth of goods.

 * ### Officers would like to identify the person pictured as part of the investigation.

Police investigating a theft in Watford have released images of a man they would like to speak to as part of their enquiries.

At around 4pm on Sunday, April 8, a woman had a money fob stolen as she shopped in the Gossamers.

The fob was then used to purchase £100 worth of goods at a Co-op in Cricklewood, London.

Officers would like to speak with the man pictured as he may have information which could assist with their investigation.

Anyone with information is asked to contact PC Mo Huzair on the non-emergency number 101, quoting crime reference C4/18/1131. You can also report information online.

Alternatively, you can contact the independent crime-fighting charity Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111 or through their Anonymous Online Form. No personal details are taken, information cannot be traced or recorded and you will never need to go to court.
