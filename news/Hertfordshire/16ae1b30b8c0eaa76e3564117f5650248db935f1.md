* ### Do you recognise these people? Police would like to speak to them as part of enquiries into a theft in Bishop's Stortford

 * ### It happened at Marks and Spencer's in South Street at around 3.15pm on Sunday, December 2

 * ### If you know those pictured, please call 101 quoting reference 41/62818/18

Officers investigating a theft from a shop in Bishop's Stortford have released images of four people they would like to speak to as part of their enquiries.

It happened at Marks and Spencer in South Street at 3.15pm on Sunday, December 2.

Two fur cardigans, two sweaters, two floral blouses, a sequin top, a further cardigan and a down jacket were stolen.

PCSO Tea Prow, from the Bishop's Stortford Safer Neighbourhood Team, said: "We would like to speak to the people pictured as we believe they may have been in the area at the time of the theft, and therefore may have information that could help progress our investigation.

"If you recognise them, or have any further information that you believe could assist police, please call the non-emergency number 101, quoting reference 41/62818/18. You can also report information online at https://www.herts.police.uk/Report or speak to one of our Force Communications Room operators via web chat online."

Alternatively, you can stay 100% anonymous by contacting the independent charity Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111 or via their untraceable online form at www.crimestoppers-uk.org. For over 30 years, Crimestoppers has always kept its promise of anonymity to everyone who contacts them.
