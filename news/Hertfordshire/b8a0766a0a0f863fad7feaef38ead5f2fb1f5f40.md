* ### Have you seen any of these items of jewellery that were stolen from a house in St Albans on June 30?

 * ### It happened in Glenlyn Avenue between 2pm and 11.30pm.

 * ### If you have been offered any of the stolen items for sale, or saw any suspicious activity in the area, please call 101 quoting crime reference 41/12193/18.

Officers investigating a burglary in St Albans have released images of some of the items stolen.

It happened in Glenlyn Avenue between 2pm and 11.30pm on Saturday, June 30.

Between these times, offender(s) gained entry to a property via the rear door and conducted a search before stealing various items of jewellery, including a number of gold rings, necklaces and earrings.

Did you see anyone acting suspiciously in the area between the times stated? Or have you been offered any items for sale that match the photographs?

Anyone with information is asked to email Detective Constable Ayodeji Afolabi, report online or by calling the non-emergency number 101, quoting crime reference 41/12193/18.

Alternatively, you can contact the independent crime-fighting charity Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111 or through their Anonymous Online Form. No personal details are taken, information cannot be traced or recorded and you will never need to go to court.
