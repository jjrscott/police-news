* ### A home in Mildred Avenue, Watford, was burgled on the afternoon of Easter Sunday.

 * ### The offender(s) took a Christmas themed 'bag for life' with a reindeer design from the property and are believed to have used it to carry stolen items from the home.

 * ### Anyone who may have seen the offender(s) in the area with a bag matching the description are asked to get in touch with officers.

Officers investigating a burglary in Watford are appealing for information and witnesses.

Between 3.30pm and 4.45pm on Easter Sunday (April 1), offender(s) gained access to a home in Mildred Avenue and stole items from inside.

Detective Constable Matt Cox from the Watford Local Crime Unit said: "We believe the offender(s) took a Christmas themed green 'bag for life' from inside the property and used it to carry the stolen items away from the home. The bag has a reindeer design on the side of it so I'm appealing for anyone who may have seen offender(s) in the area around the times stated with a bag matching the description to get in touch as you may have information that could assist our investigation."

The following items were also stolen:

 * Amazon Fire HD.
 * Amazon Fire HD (children's version)
 * Xbox game console.
 * Samsung Galaxy S5 mobile phone.
 * Apple iPhone 7
 * Gold plated bangles
 * A pink flower design vanity bag.
 * Various toiletries.
 * iPhone SE.
 * Remington shaver.
 * Girl's watch.
 * A driving licence.

Anyone with information is asked to contact Det Con Cox by emailing, call the non-emergency number 101, quoting crime reference C1/18/1884 or report information online.

Alternatively, you can contact the independent crime-fighting charity Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111 or through their Anonymous Online Form. No personal details are taken, information cannot be traced or recorded and you will never need to go to court.
