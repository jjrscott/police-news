Officers are appealing for information and witnesses following an alleged robbery in Waltham Cross.

The incident is reported to have happened in New Ford Road at around 4.40pm yesterday (Thursday, March 21).

The victim - a 22 year old man, was reportedly approached by two males who seriously assaulted him before taking his bag.

Detective Constable Michael Doe from the Broxbourne Local Crime Unit said: "The victim was taken to hospital for treatment and I'm glad to say he is now recovering.

"I would like to appeal to anyone who might have seen what happened, or anyone who has useful information, to please get in touch.

"If you think you can help, please call the non-emergency number 101 or email me at _michael.doe@herts.pnn.police.uk_ quoting crime reference 41/26412/19."

You can also report information online at https://www.herts.police.uk/Report.

Alternatively, you can stay 100% anonymous by contacting the independent charity Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111 or via their untraceable online form at www.crimestoppers-uk.org.

 * A 28 year old man from London has been arrested on suspicion of robbery and GBH. He is in police custody at this time.
