Officers in Stevenage are appealing for the public's help to trace a man they believe could help them with their investigations into charity box thefts in the town.

Prior to and on Remembrance Sunday, six British Legion charity boxes were stolen from locations around Stevenage Town Centre.

 * Natwest on November 8 at 12:43pm
 * The Cornish Pantry on November 8 at 3pm
 * Barclays Bank on November 8 at 2.15pm
 * The Garden House Hospice on November 9 at 12.30pm
 * Mecca Bingo on November 9 at 12noon
 * Wetherspoons (The Standard Bearer) on November 11 at 3:21pm

Police would like to speak to the man in the picture as they believe he was in the area at the time and may have information which could help the investigation.

If you recognise him or have any other information about the incident please contact PCSO Christie Hoare via the non-emergency number 101. You can also report information online at www.herts.police.uk/Report.

Alternatively, you can stay 100% anonymous by contacting the independent charity Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111 or via their untraceable online form at www.crimestoppers-uk.org. In over 30 years, Crimestoppers charity has always kept its promise of anonymity to everyone who contacts them.
