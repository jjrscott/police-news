Police have released photos of vehicles and bikes, that are reported to have been taken during a burglary in Redbourn, in a bid to trace them.

Between 9pm yesterday (Saturday, January 26) and 8.30am this morning (Sunday, January 27), offender(s) are alleged to have gained access to an address in Flamsteadbury Lane before taking numerous property:

 * Two black Toyota Hiluxes (pictured)
 * Two trailers
 * Three off road bikes (two are pictured)
 * A black Golf GTI
 * Welders
 * Generators
 * A Karcher pressure washer
 * Three Snap-on tool boxes containing tools

A Mercedes Sprinter that was also reported to have been taken has been recovered in Luton this morning.

Anyone who has seen the vehicles or bikes pictured should get in touch on the non-emergency number 101 quoting crime reference 41/8718/19. Similarly, any witnesses or anyone with other information that could help officers should get in touch on 101 quoting the crime reference.

You can also report information online at https://www.herts.police.uk/Report.

Alternatively, you can stay 100% anonymous by contacting the independent charity Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111 or via their untraceable online form at www.crimestoppers-uk.org. In over 30 years, Crimestoppers charity has always kept its promise of anonymity to everyone who contacts them.

 * A 46 year old man from Luton has been arrested on suspicion of burglary. He remains in police custody at this time.
