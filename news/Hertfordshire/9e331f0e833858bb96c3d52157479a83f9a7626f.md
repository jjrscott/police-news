* Appeal for information following a robbery in Abbots Langley
 * Victim had her Rolex watch stolen
 * Offender rode off on a push bike

Police are investigating a robbery which occurred yesterday afternoon (26th June) in Abbots Langley.

At around 5pm the victim, a 61 year old woman, was approached from behind on South Way and grabbed by the offender who has demanded her watch. The victim has attempted to pull away and the offender has managed to take her watch, a women's 18 carat gold Rolex. He then rode off on a push bike towards Hunton Bridge.

The suspect is described as a white boy aged around 14-15 years old, 5ft 6/7ins tall, with dark hair and of a slim build. He was wearing dark trainers, a navy hooded top and dark jogging bottoms.

Anyone who was in the area at the time of the incident and saw what happened or who saw anyone matching the description of the offender should please contact Investigator Charlie McLaughlin by calling the non-emergency number 101, quoting crime reference number 41/11417/18 or report information online at https://www.herts.police.uk/Report.

Alternatively, you can contact the independent crime-fighting charity Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111 or through their Anonymous Online Form at www.crimestoppers-uk.org. No personal details are taken, information cannot be traced or recorded and you will never need to go to court.
