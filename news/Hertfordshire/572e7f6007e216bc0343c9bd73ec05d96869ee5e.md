The Welwyn Hatfield Safer Neighbourhood Team (SNT) have set new priorities to focus on over the next three months.

The SNT are responsible for fixing short- and long-term neighbourhood issues such as anti-social behaviour and they work with local partners, including the council, to ensure that Welwyn Hatfield is a safe place to live and work.

Inspector Kash Hussain said: "We are determined to make sure that Welwyn Hatfield remains a safe and pleasant place and I would like to thank everyone who got in touch using our public feedback service echo to let us know their views. It's really useful to understand what you would like to see from us and your feedback has been used to set these priorities.

"We will review these again in three months but in the meantime, you can still provide us with feedback using echo. Simply go to _bit.ly/police-welhat_ and have your say."

The following priorities have been set for Welwyn Hatfield:

_Welwyn Hatfield North_

(Welwyn West, Welwyn East, Haldens, Sherrards, Handside, Peartree and Panshanger)

 * Anti-social behaviour in Welwyn Garden City town centre
 * Bicycle thefts in Welwyn Garden City town centre
 * Drugs and drug-related anti-social behaviour in Haldens and Panshanger

_Welwyn Hatfield South_

(Hollybush, Cuffley, Howlands, Northaw, Hatfield East, Hatfield South, Welham Green, Brookmans Park and Little Heath)

 * Anti-social behaviour in King George V Playing Fields
 * Speeding in Brookmans Park
 * Parking in Hatfield South

_Welwyn Hatfield West_

_ _

(Hatfield Villages, Hatfield Central, Hatfield South West and the University of Hertfordshire)

 * Anti-social behaviour in Mosquito Way
 * Bike thefts in Hatfield Town Centre
 * Anti-social behaviour in Roe Hill Park
