Officers are releasing a picture of a bicycle that has been stolen in Stevenage and are appealing for information and witnesses.

The bike was allegedly taken from a garden in Bradman Way at around 1am on July 9.

A few minutes later it was reportedly seen being ridden through the Oval shopping centre by a female who was with a group of men.

The bicycle is a B'Twin men's mountain bike.

Anyone who saw the alleged offence or has any information that could assist the investigation is asked to contact PC Andrew Burnett by emailing andrew.burnett@herts.pnn.police.uk, calling the non-emergency number 101, quoting crime reference 41/15754/18 or report information online at https://www.herts.police.uk/Report.

Alternatively, you can contact the independent crime-fighting charity Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111 or through their Anonymous Online Form at www.crimestoppers-uk.org. No personal details are taken, information cannot be traced or recorded and you will never need to go to court.

 * An 18 year old man from Stevenage has been arrested on suspicion of theft. He has been released under investigating while enquiries continue.
