* ### Police would like to speak to the man pictured as part of their enquiries into a burglary in St Albans

 * ### A quantity of cash was taken from a safe in The Snug in French Row on February 2

 * ### If you recognise him, please contact police quoting crime reference 41/10707/19

Officers investigating a burglary in St Albans have released an image of a man they would like to speak to as part of their enquiries.

It happened at The Snug in French Row between 1.20pm and 1.45pm on Saturday, February 2.

Offender(s) gained entry to a staff-only area and searched through coats to find a key for a safe.

They opened the safe and stole hundreds of pounds in cash before leaving the bar.

PC James McAlister, who is investigating, said: "We are keen to identify the person pictured as we believe he may have information that could help progress our investigation."

If you recognise this person, or have any further information, please contact PC McAlister on _james.mcalister@herts.pnn.police.uk_ , report information online at www.herts.police.uk/Report or speak to an operator in our Force Communications Room via online web chat, quoting reference 41/10707/19.

Online web chat can be launched here: https://www.herts.police.uk/Information-and-services/About-us/Contact

You can also call the non-emergency number 101, and ask for Herts Police.

Alternatively, you can stay 100% anonymous by contacting the independent charity Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111 or via their untraceable online form at www.crimestoppers-uk.org.
