Officers investigating the theft of a purse in Abbots Langley have released two images of a woman they would like to speak to as part of their enquiries.

It happened at St Francis Hospice shop on the High Street between 2.30pm and 2.45pm on January 10.

The victim, a woman in her 60s, was in the shop when she realised her purse was missing before reporting it to police.

PCSO Nathan Law, from the Abbots Langley and Chorleywood Safer Neighbourhood Team, said: "Do you recognise the woman pictured? We are keen to identify her and speak to her as we believe she may have information that could help progress our investigation."

If you know the woman pictured, or have any further information, please contact PCSO Law by emailing, report information online or speak to an operator in our Force Communications Room via online web chat, quoting reference 41/3081/19.

Online web chat can be launched here.

You can also call the non-emergency number 101, and ask for Herts Police.

Alternatively, you can stay 100% anonymous by contacting the independent charity Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111 or via their untraceable online form.
