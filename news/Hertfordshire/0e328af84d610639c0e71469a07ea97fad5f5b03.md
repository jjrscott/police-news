Police are appealing for witnesses and information after a man was bitten by a dog in Letchworth.

The incident happened on 5th July at 7.45am in The Greenway, Wilbury Hills Road near to the Wilbury pub.

The victim, a man in his 60s, had been walking his Retriever cross at the location when a dog, described as a black, brown and white medium sized Collie, ran up to him and bit him on the leg. He has suffered bruising to his leg.

The owner of the dog who bit the man is described as a white man in his late 40s to early 50s who was of medium build. He was described to be balding and wearing shorts and a pink T-shirt.

Anyone with information is asked to contact PC Charlotte Boot by calling the non-emergency number 101, quoting crime reference number 41/14633/18, or report information online

Alternatively, you can contact the independent crime-fighting charity Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111 or through their Anonymous Online Form. No personal details are taken, information cannot be traced or recorded and you will never need to go to court.
