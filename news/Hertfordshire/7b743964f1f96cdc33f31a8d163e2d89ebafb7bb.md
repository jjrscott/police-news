* ### A man was assaulted in Amwell Road, Great Amwell

 * ### The incident happened at around 3am on Saturday, May 25

 * ### The victim suffered a fractured skull and lacerations

 * ### Anyone with information is asked to contact Detective Constable Narinder Padda

Police are appealing for information and witnesses following an incident in Great Amwell.

At around 3am on Saturday (May 25) the victim - a man aged in his 20s - was walking along Amwell Road when he was approached by two men who assaulted him.

The first offender is described as being white, aged around 25 years old, around 5ft 10in tall with dark hair.

Detective Constable Narinder Padda, who is investigating, said: "The victim, who required hospital treatment, suffered a fractured skull and several lacerations, some of which required stitches.

"I'm appealing for anyone who witnessed the incident or has information that could assist the investigation to please get in touch."

Anyone with information can contact DC Padda by emailing narinder.padda@herts.pnn.police.uk, report information online at www.herts.police.uk/Report, speak to an operator in our Force Communications Room via our online web chat, which can be launched here: herts.police.uk/contact or call the non-emergency number 101, quoting crime reference 41/46775/19.

Alternatively, you can stay 100% anonymous by contacting the independent charity Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111 or via their untraceable online form at www.crimestoppers-uk.org.
