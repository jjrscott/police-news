 * ### The vehicle failed to stop at the scene.

 * ### Officers would like to speak to anyone who saw the incident or has information that could help identify the driver of the vehicle.

 * ### The woman suffered cuts and bruising and has since been discharged from hospital.

Police are appealing for witnesses and information following a fail to stop collision in Hemel Hempstead on Monday, September 24.

At around 4.30pm a woman pedestrian was crossing Adeyfield Road at the zebra crossing when she was hit by a dark coloured car. The driver of the vehicle failed to stop at the scene.

The woman, aged in her 30s, was taken to Watford General Hospital. She suffered cuts and bruising and has since been discharged.

PC Sam Swann, who is investigating, said: "We have carried out significant enquiries since this collision and are pursuing a number of lines of enquiry.

"I'm appealing for anyone who witnessed the incident and has not yet spoken to police to get in touch. I'd also like to hear from anyone who believes they know who was driving the vehicle at the time of the collision or has any information that could assist the on-going investigation to make contact."

Anyone with information can contact PC Swann by emailing _sam.swann@herts.pnn.police.uk_ , calling the non-emergency number quoting ISR 542 of 24/9/18 or report information online at www.herts.police.uk/Report.

Alternatively, contact the independent crime-fighting charity Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111 or through their anonymous online form at www.crimestoppers-uk.org. No personal details are taken, information cannot be traced or recorded and you will never need to go to court.
