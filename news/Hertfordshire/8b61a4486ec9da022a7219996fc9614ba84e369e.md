* ### Police are investigating a collision involving a a cyclist and a Fiat 500 in Stevenage

 * ### It happened in Monkswood Way around 4.40pm on Thursday, January 4

 * ### Anyone with information should contact Sergeant Paul Stanbridge on the non-emergency number 101

Police investigating a serious road traffic collision in Stevenage yesterday (Thursday, January 4) are appealing for witnesses to come forward.

The incident occurred in Monkswood Way, at the junction with the entrance to Asda car park, at around 4.40pm.

For reasons unknown at this stage a Fiat 500 was in collision with a cyclist.

The cyclist, a man aged in his 30s, sustained life-threatening injuries and was taken to Addenbrooke's Hospital where he remains at this time.

The driver of the Fiat was uninjured.

Police are appealing for anyone who witnessed the incident, or events leading up to it, to please get in touch.

Anyone with information is asked to contact Sergeant Paul Stanbridge at the Bedfordshire, Cambridgeshire and Hertfordshire Road Policing Unit on paul.stanbridge@herts.pnn.police.uk or the non-emergency number 101, quoting ISR 514 of January 4. You can also report information online at https://www.herts.police.uk/Report .

Alternatively, you can contact the independent crime-fighting charity Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111 or through their Anonymous Online Form at www.crimestoppers-uk.org. No personal details are taken, information cannot be traced or recorded and you will never need to go to court.
