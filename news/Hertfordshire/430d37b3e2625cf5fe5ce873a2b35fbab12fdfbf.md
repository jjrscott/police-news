Officers investigating an incident in Watford are asking for witnesses and information to assist with their enquiry.

The incident occurred after a disagreement over a parking space at Morrisons in Ascot Road, Watford, at around 8.50am on Thursday October 18.

It is reported that following the argument a knife was produced and the victim fled the scene.

PC Krisztina Gulyas from the Case Investigation Team, who is investigating, said: "This incident took place during a weekday morning, when there were would have been a lot of people around, either shopping, doing the school run or on the way to work, so there is likely to be several witnesses to this incident.

"If you have any information you believe may be useful, please call the non-emergency number 101 or report information online at www.herts.police.uk/Report, quoting reference 41/47196/18."

Alternatively, you can contact the independent crime-fighting charity Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111 or through their Anonymous Online Form at www.crimestoppers-uk.org. No personal details are taken, information cannot be traced or recorded and you will never need to go to court.

 * A 37 -year-old man from Lincolnshire and a 38-year-old man from Germany, have been arrested on suspicion of possession of an offensive weapon in connection with this incident. They have been released under investigation while enquiries continue.
