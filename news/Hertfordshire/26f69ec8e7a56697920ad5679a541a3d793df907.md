* ### Man suffered serious injuries following a collision in Goff's Oak

 * ### He was assisting a lorry driver when he became trapped between the vehicle and a gate

 * ### If you witnessed the incident, or saw anything that could assist the police investigation, please call 101 quoting ISR 383 of October 30

Officers investigating a collision in Goff's Oak are appealing for information and witnesses.

It happened just before 2.30pm yesterday (Monday, October 30) on Silver Street, outside Silver Street Cemetery.

The victim, a man in his 40s, was assisting a lorry driver when he became trapped between the vehicle and an iron gate, causing serious injuries.

He was airlifted to a London hospital for further treatment and remains there today (Tuesday, October 31). His injuries are not thought to be life-threatening.

Sergeant William Hood, from the Bedfordshire, Cambridgeshire and Hertfordshire Road Policing Unit, said: "We are appealing to anyone who may have witnessed the incident, or the aftermath, to please come forward and speak to us if they have not done so already."

If you have any further information that could assist the investigation, please call Hertfordshire Constabulary on the non-emergency number 101, quoting ISR 383 of October 30 or report information online

Alternatively, if you have any information about this incident you can contact the independent crime-fighting charity Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111 or through their Anonymous Online Form. No personal details are taken, information cannot be traced or recorded and you will never need to go to court.
