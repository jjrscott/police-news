The A12 near Wangford has now reopened following a serious road traffic collision.

Police were called just before 10:30am this morning, Thursday 19 January, to reports of a two vehicle collision between a black Nissan Qashqai and a blue Volvo XC70.

Three people sustained injuries and were taken to James Paget Hospital by road ambulance, however these are not believed to be life changing or life threatening.

The road was closed whilst a collision investigation took place and for recovery of the vehicles to be arranged, but has now fully reopened in both directions.

Officers are appealing for any witnesses or anyone who may have seen either vehicle immediately prior to the collision to call the Serious Collision Investigation Team on **101** quoting reference CAD 112 of 19 January.

## Previous release | 19 January 2017

Emergency services are currently on the scene of a serious collision on the A12 near Wangford.

Police were called just before 10:30am this morning, Thursday 19 January, to reports of a two vehicle collision on the A12.

The air ambulance and fire service are in attendance and at present the road remains closed both ways. Local diversions are being put in place, however officers are advising that motorists to avoid the area at the moment if possible.

Officers are asking anyone who may have seen the collision to call Suffolk Police on **101** , quoting CAD 112 of today, Thursday 19 January.
