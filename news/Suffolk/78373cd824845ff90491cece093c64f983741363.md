Police are appealing for witnesses following a robbery that took place in Ipswich at around 1am on Sunday, 12 March.

An 18-year-old man walking along Kestrel Road, near the junction of Whinchat Close, was approached by three men. The suspects pulled out two knives and demanded that the victim hand over his valuables. The offenders stole his wallet and mobile phone before making off in the direction of Hawthorn Drive.

The first suspect is described as white, around 18 - 20 years’ old, approximately 6ft in height and of skinny build. He was wearing dark clothing, including a hoody which partially covered his face.

The second suspect is also described as white, around 19 – 20 year’s old and of skinny build. He is described as slightly shorter than the first suspect and was wearing dark jeans and a hoody.

The third suspect is described as being of bigger build to the others and was he was also wearing dark jeans and hoody.

Witnesses or anybody who has any information related to the incident are asked to call Ipswich CID on **101** reference 22392/17. Alternatively contact Crimestoppers anonymously on **0800 555 111** or through their anonymous online form at www.crimestoppers-uk.org.
