The A140 at Mendlesham has now partially reopened following a serious road traffic collision.

Police were called at 12pm this afternoon, Monday 12 September, to reports of a collision between a car and a lorry on the A140.

One casualty was taken to Addenbrookeâ€™s Hospital by the air ambulance having suffered serious injuries and two further casualties were taken to Ipswich Hospital.

The road remained closed whilst a collision investigation took place and for recovery of the vehicles to be arranged.

One lane has now reopened, with traffic being allowed to pass through in both directions by the use of stop-go boards. The road is estimated to fully reopen at around 6pm.

Any witnesses are asked to call the Serious Collision Investigation Team on **101** quoting reference CAD 154 of 12 September.

## Previous release - 12/09/2016

Emergency services are currently at the scene of a road traffic collision in Mendlesham.

Police were called at 12pm this afternoon, Monday 12 September, to reports of a collision between a car and a lorry on the A140.

The A140 is currently completely blocked, with ambulance and fire crews also in attendance and motorists are advised to avoid the area if possible.

Any witnesses to the collision are asked to call Suffolk Police on **101** quoting reference CAD 154 of 12 September.
