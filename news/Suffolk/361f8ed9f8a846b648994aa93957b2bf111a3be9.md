Officers are reminding the public to keep their keys out of sight after two burglaries in Ipswich over the weekend.

The first incident took place on Tranmere Grove sometime between 2am and 4.45am in the early hours of Saturday 19 May. Offender/s entered the house and removed two sets of vehicle keys from a window sill before then stealing an white Audi Quattro car (registration plate BG58 BMX) and a black Audi A1 (registration plate AY13 GUC) Nothing else was stolen.

The second burglary took place on Ludlow Close sometime between 1.15am and 7.15am on Sunday 20 May. In this incident offender/s entered the house and removed house and car keys before stealing a black BMW X3 (registration plate AV66 FZF) and a blue BMW 325CI M Sport coupe car (registration plate LF04 XAZ) Other items stolen include an orange handbag, a grey purse containing cash and bank cards and an iPhone 5S.

Anybody who saw anything suspicious at the times above or has any information regarding the burglaries are asked to contact Police on 101 quoting reference 27197/18 (Tranmere Grove) or 27397/18 (Ludlow Close) Alternatively contact the charity Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111.

Police advise that when at home members of the public should keep all keys out of sight and away from doors and windows.
