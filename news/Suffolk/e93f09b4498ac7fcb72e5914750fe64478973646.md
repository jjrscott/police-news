Police have arrested two people in connection with an incident of grievous bodily harm that took place in Ipswich.

It happened in Marigold Avenue last week at about 10.40pm on Wednesday 29 April when two males attended a residential premises.

A male victim, aged in his 40s, sustained stab wounds below his right shoulder blade and sustained a cut on his head.. He was taken to hospital for treatment to his non-life-threatening and non life-changing injuries.

Two men have now been arrested. One male, aged 20 was arrested on Saturday 2 May on suspicion of GBH and taken to Martlesham Police Investigation Centre for questioning. He was subsequently released under investigation, pending further enquiries.

A 34 year old man was arrested this morning (Tues 5 May) also on suspicion of GBH and he is currently at Martlesham Police Investigation Centre for questioning.

Anyone who has knowledge of the incident or saw any suspicious activity in the area should contact Ipswich CID, quoting crime reference 37/23882/20

## Previous release I 30 April 2020

Police are appealing for witnesses to an incident of grievous bodily harm that took place in Ipswich.

It happened in Marigold Avenue last night at about 10.40pm (Wednesday 29 April).

Two males, who were wearing balaclavas, attended a residential premises where it was reported an argument took place between a male at the property and the two males.

The male victim, aged in his 40s, sustained stab wounds below his right shoulder blade, was slashed on his head, was beaten on his leg and he also sustained a wound on his wrist. He was taken to hospital for treatment where his injuries were described as being non-life-threatening or life-changing.

The suspects were believed to have fled in the direction of Hawthorne Avenue. Officers believe it was a targeted incident.

Anyone who has knowledge of the incident or saw any suspicious activity in the area should contact Ipswich CID, quoting crime reference 37/23882/20
