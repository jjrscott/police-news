Police are appealing for witnesses to a road traffic collision that occurred in Ipswich.

It happened on Northgate Street on Saturday 1 September at about 3.40pm.

A 61 year old pedestrian was in collision with a pedal cyclist outside the Cash Converters store. The cycle is described as a black and grey Boardman model.

The female pedestrian sustained serious head injuries as a result of the collision, and was initially taken to Ipswich hospital, before being transferred to Addenbrookes hospital, Cambridge, where her condition is described as serious.

The pedal cyclist was uninjured in the collision.

Police are asking for any witnesses to the collision, or anyone who may have dashcam footage or mobile phone footage of the collision to contact Suffolk roads policing unit at Police Headquarters on 101, quoting CAD 277 of 1 September 2018.
