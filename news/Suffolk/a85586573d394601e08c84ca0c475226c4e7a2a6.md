Police are appealing for information following burglaries at two adjoining properties in Red Lodge.

Burglars smashed French doors at one of the semi-detached homes in Warren Road between 6.30am on Tuesday 17 April and 10.30am on Wednesday 18 April. Nothing is believed to have been stolen.

The adjoining home was burgled between Monday 16 April and Wednesday 18 April after a rear kitchen window was smashed. It is not known if anything was stolen.

Police are linking the burglaries and have issued descriptions of three male suspects.

The first is described as white with a tanned complexion, aged in his late 20s, of stocky build, 5ft 9in tall with black hair visible under a blue baseball cap and wearing a grey sweatshirt.

The second man is white, aged in his early 20s, of slim build with short gingery/blonde hair. He was wearing a white T-shirt with a pattern or design on the front.

The third man is described as white, aged in his early 20s, 5ft 9in tall, of average build and wearing a baseball cap and grey sweatshirt.

Anyone who saw any suspicious activity during the times stated or believes they have information relating to the incident should contact West CID at Bury St Edmunds police station on 101, quoting crime reference 37/20818/18 or 37/20896/18. Alternatively, they can call the charity Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555111.
