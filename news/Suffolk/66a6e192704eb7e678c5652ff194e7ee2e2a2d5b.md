Three men have been remanded in custody after being charged in connection following two incidents of aggravated burglary in Lowestoft.

Police were called at approximately 10:35pm on Friday 9 December to reports that offenders had broken into two flats at a communal residence in St Peters Street and assaulted the occupants.

On arrival officers discovered a man in his 30s and another in his 40s had sustained facial injuries. Both required hospital treatment.

On Tuesday, 12 December, police arrested three people - all from Lowestoft - in connection with the incident.

Jack Molyneux, aged 27 and of Park Road, Daniel Cain, aged 24 and of Raglan Street, and Zac Thackery, aged 25 and of Montgomery Avenue, have subsequently been charged with two counts of aggravated burglary.
