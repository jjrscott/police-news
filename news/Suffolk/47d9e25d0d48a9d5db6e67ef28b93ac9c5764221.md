A 36 year-old Lowestoft woman has been sentenced today, Wednesday 6 September, to 21 months imprisonment after attacking a man in Lowestoft, leaving him with life-changing injuries.

Leanne Rowett of Short Lane, Carlton Colville was charged with GBH after an incident at a public house on London Road South in the early hours of Sunday 17 July 2016.

During the incident a 28-year-old man was pushed backwards causing him to fall over a chair onto the floor. He was then kicked and punched whilst on the floor. The victim sustained a fractured eye socket and nose, and cuts to his face. As a result of the attack the victim has permanently loss the sight in one eye.

Rowett pleaded guilty and was sentenced today at Ipswich Crown Court.

Gareth Rowett, aged 37 of Beresford Road, Lowestoft also pleaded guilty to assaulting the victim’s friend during the same incident. Gareth Rowett was given a 12 month community order and will have to carry out 80 hours of unpaid work.

Detective Constable Mei-Lin Chan said: "This was a violent attack that has left a young man with life-changing injuries. Offences like this will not be tolerated and we will make every effort to put offenders before the courts.”
