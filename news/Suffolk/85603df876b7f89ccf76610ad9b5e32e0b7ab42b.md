A 20-year-old man, Billy Wilson of Hill Rise, Stowmarket and a 17-year-old boy, also from the town, have both been charged in connection with two robberies that took place in Ipswich earlier this month.

The charges relate to an incident that took place on Byron Road, in the town, at around 9.45pm on Tuesday 2 January. Two men on foot reported being approached by two males who then robbed them both. One victim had his mobile phone stolen, the other man had a pair of headphones stolen. Both victims sustained minor injuries.

Billy Wilson has also been charged with
• Aggravated vehicle taking from an address in Stowmarket on 7 January and causing damage to the car
• Dangerous driving and driving whilst disqualified on the same day in Ipswich.
• Causing criminal damage of a police cell at the Police Investigation Centre, on 12 January.

He has been remanded in custody to appear before Ipswich Crown Court on 2 February 2018.

The 17-year-boy has also been charged with
• Criminal damage of a motorbike in Stowmarket, which took place on 2 January.
• Attempting to enter a dwelling on Macaulay Road, Ipswich with intent to steal on 3 January.
• Breaching conditions of a criminal behaviour order which prohibited him from entering Ipswich.

He has been remanded into youth custody and is due to appear before Ipswich Magistrates Court on 17 January 2018.
