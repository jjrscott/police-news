Police are keen to speak to two women who may have witnessed a stabbing in Newmarket last night.

Officers received a report at about 10.25pm last night (Monday 21 May) after a man reported he had been stabbed in Church Lane near to Turner Hall.

The 18 year old victim had sustained a serious injury to his abdomen, although his injuries are not thought to be life threatening.

Police are in the early stages of their investigation however enquiries carried out so far suggest this may have been a targeted attack.

Anybody who witnessed the incident or who has any information are asked to call Suffolk police on 101, quoting reference 37/27774/18. Alternatively, call the charity Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111 or through their anonymous online form at www.crimestoppers-uk.org.

## Previous release I Tuesday 22 May

Police are appealing for witnesses after a stabbing in Newmarket last night.

Officers received a report at about 10.25pm last night (Monday 21 May) after a man reported he had been stabbed in Church Lane near to Turner Hall.

The 18 year old victim had sustained a serious injury to his abdomen and was taken to Addenbrooke's hospital for treatment where he remains. His injuries are not thought to be life threatening.

Police are in the early stages of their investigation however enquiries carried out so far suggest this may have been a targeted attack.

Anybody who witnessed the incident or who has any information are asked to call Suffolk police on 101, quoting reference 37/27774/18. Alternatively, call the charity Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111 or through their anonymous online form at www.crimestoppers-uk.org.
