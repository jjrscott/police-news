Officers are appealing for witnesses following reports of four burglaries at business premises in the Sudbury area.

The first incident occurred sometime between 11pm on Monday 29 August and 8am and Tuesday 30 August at a premises just off Westgate Street in Long Melford. Offender/s entered the building and stole a safe containing a quantity of cash.

In the second burglary, which took place around 10.50pm on Tuesday 30 August at a premises in Newton close to the A134, suspect/s forced entry to an outbuilding and stole a petrol hedge trimmer.

On Thursday 1 September at around 11.25pm at a business premises on Hunts Hill, Glemsford, entry was forced by unknown suspect/s and a quantity of cash was stolen. Two gaming machines were also damaged. A witness reported seeing a dark, blue or black, Ford Mondeo shape car driving down Hunts Hill towards the A1092 shortly after the incident.

Finally, also on Thursday 1 September, at around midnight, offender/s forced entry to a business premises on Joes Road, Great Cornard. It is understood that a ride on mower or similar was stolen.

Police are investigating possible links between these burglaries. Officers are advising the public and business owners to be particularly vigilant if they see or hear anything suspicious in their neighbourhood.

Witnesses or anybody who saw anything suspicious at the time are asked to call Suffolk Police on 101 reference 52839/16 (Long Melford) 52804/16 (Newton) 53207/16 (Glemsford) or 53205/16 (Great Cornard) Alternatively contact Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111 or through their anonymous online form at www.crimestoppers-uk.org.
