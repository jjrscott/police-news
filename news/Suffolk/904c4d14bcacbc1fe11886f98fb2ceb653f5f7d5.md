Police are appealing for witnesses to an assault that took place at Lowestoft.

It took place on Sunday 11 November at about 1.30am on the rear patio of the Shish Bar and Restaurant on the Lower Esplanade.

A verbal altercation took place between the victim who was man in his 50s and the suspect. The victim was assaulted, causing him to fall and hit the back of his head on concrete.

The male received serious head injuries and was taken to Addenbrookesâ€™ hospital where he remains.

A 22 year old man was arrested yesterday (Wednesday 14 November) in connection with the incident on suspicion of assault and taken to Great Yarmouth Police Investigation Centre for questioning. He has since been released under investigation pending further enquiries.

Police are appealing for witnesses who may have witnessed the assault, or witnessed the disturbance in the bar area, prior to the incident at the rear of the premises. They should contact Lowestoft CID, quoting crime reference 37/65313/18.

Alternatively, contact the independent charity Crimestoppers 100% anonymously on 0800 555 111 or via their anonymous online form at crimestoppers-uk.org.
