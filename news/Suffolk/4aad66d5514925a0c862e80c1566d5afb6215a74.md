Police are appealing for witnesses to a robbery that took place in Ipswich.

It happened Saturday 17 November at the McColls store on Maidenhall Approach at just before 8pm. The two suspects have entered armed with a knife and baseball bat. Staff have been threatened by suspects who have made off with cash and cigarettes.

One offender was wearing a black puffa jacket, as well as a cap with red writing on as well as grey tracksuit bottoms and yellow gloves. The other suspect was aged 18 to 25 years old, wore a blue or black puffa jacket with a white motif, had grey tracksuit bottoms on, and wore black trainers and black gloves.. He was also wearing a grey balaclava and had a black rucksack with white reflective straps. Both suspects were described as being white.

Enquiries are on-going and anyone who witnessed the incident, saw any suspicious behaviour prior to the incident or afterwards, or has further knowledge of the robbery that could assist should contact Ipswich CID on 101, quoting crime reference 66645/18.

Alternatively, contact the independent charity Crimestoppers 100% anonymously on 0800 555 111 or via their anonymous online form at crimestoppers-uk.org.
