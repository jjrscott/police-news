Police investigating a stabbing in Ipswich have released a 15-year-old girl from custody who was arrested yesterday (Sunday 16 December).

Police were called just after 5.30am to reports that two men had been stabbed at a property in Moore Road.

The victims, both 21-years-old, were taken by land ambulance to Ipswich Hospital and were subsequently discharged later the same day.

A 15-year girl, who was arrested on suspicion of wounding with intent, has been released under investigation pending further enquiries.

Officers believe that this was a targeted attack and as such there is no wider threat to the community.

This incident is also not connected to the fatal stabbing in Turin Street, Ipswich, which took place yesterday afternoon.

Any witnesses to the incident or persons who were in the area with dash cameras are asked to contact South CID at Landmark House on 101 quoting crime reference 37/72554/18.

Alternatively, contact the charity Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111 or through their anonymous online form at www.crimestoppers-uk.org.
