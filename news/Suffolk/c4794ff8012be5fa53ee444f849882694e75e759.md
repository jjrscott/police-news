Police are appealing for witnesses after two 17-year-old boys were injured during a stabbing incident in Ipswich.

The attack took place between 9pm and 9.15pm last night, Monday 3 October, in Whitland Close.

The first victim was approached in the street by two men, one of whom then stabbed him in upper back. He has received treatment in hospital and his condition is currently described as stable.

The second victim attempted to intervene and suffered a cut to the back of his head and a serious hand injury. He is undergoing hospital treatment, with the injury to his hand described as potentially life-changing.

The suspect is described as 5ft 9in tall, dressed in black and wearing a balaclava. He was accompanied by another man who was also wearing a balaclava.

Detective Chief Inspector Barry Byford said: "We are treating this as an isolated incident and do not believe there is any wider threat to the community. However, we will be increasing patrols in the area to provide reassurance to local residents.

"There were a number of people in the vicinity of the attack at the time it occurred and so I am appealing for any witnesses to come forward.‚Äù
