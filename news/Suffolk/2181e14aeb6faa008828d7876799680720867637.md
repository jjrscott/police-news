A missing 13-year-old girl from Ixworth Thorpe, near Bury St Edmunds, has been found safe and well.

Alice Brindley had last been seen at Cambridge railway station at around 6.30pm yesterday, Sunday 19 March, from where she travelled to London.

Police enquiries into her whereabouts had been taking place over the past 24 hours, and as a result of these Alice was found in Islington this evening, Monday 20 March. She is now being brought home by Suffolk Constabulary officers.

Police would like to thank members of the public and the media for their assistance with this appeal.

## Previous release | 8.32am 20 March 2017

Police are appealing for help in tracing a missing 13-year-old girl from Ixworth Thorpe near Bury St Edmunds.

Alice Brindley was last seen at Cambridge train station at around 6:30pm yesterday, Sunday 19 March, and has not been seen since. It is believed Alice has headed to London and may currently be in the Islington area.

Alice is described as white, with collar length, red hair. When she was last seen she was wearing black skinny jeans, a black hoodie, a grey sheepskin top underneath the hoodie and black trainers. Alice was carrying a black leather rucksack with gold coloured zips.

Officers are concerned for her welfare and are asking anyone who has seen Alice, or who has any information on where she may be, to contact Suffolk Police on 101.
