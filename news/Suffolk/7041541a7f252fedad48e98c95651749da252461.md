Police have charged a 29-year-old man with attempted grievous bodily harm and possession of an offensive weapon.

Officers were called at around 10am yesterday, Tuesday 24 April, to reports of a street disturbance in the Beaconsfield Road area of the town. One man was seen to assault another man by hitting him on the head with a hammer.

Police were immediately despatched and located the victim, a man in his thirties, who at this time declined medical attention.

Officers conducted enquiries at the scene and a short time later arrested a man in connection with the incident.

Nathan Baldry of Beaconsfield Road, Lowestoft has been charged with attempted grievous bodily harm and possession of an offensive weapon.

Baldry has been bailed to appear before Great Yarmouth Magistrates Court on 23 May.
