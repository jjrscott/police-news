Police are appealing for witnesses after two burglaries at the same location near Thrandeston.

Sometime on Sunday 18 November at a property in the vicinity of the A143, two old traditional milk churns were stolen from outside and two stainless steel water jugs were taken from within. Two canaries (one orange and one white) were also taken from an aviary.

Yesterday Monday 19 November, sometime between 8.30am and 5.30pm, two whippet/greyhound cross breed type dogs were stolen from the same address. The older dog, a female, is thought be around seven or eight years old and her puppy is aged around nine months. The younger dog has been micro-chipped. Two further birds, a goldfinch and a mule bird were stolen from the aviary and some Imari Crown Derby pottery items and jewellery taken from within.

Witnesses or anybody who has any information or who been offered the dogs or birds are asked to call Police on 101 quoting reference 67029/18. Alternatively, call the charity Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111.
