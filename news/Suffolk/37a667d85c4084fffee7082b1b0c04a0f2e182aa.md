Officers are appealing for information or witnesses following two burglaries that took place at the weekend in Stanton and Mendlesham.

The first incident took place at Stanton Community Village Hall, Old Bury Road in Stanton on Saturday 3 September at approximately 00.20am. Suspects forced entry to the building and stole an unknown quantity of alcohol. A television screen was smashed and several cupboards were also damaged.

It is thought that the offenders may have been carrying axes and sledgehammers. The suspects, believed to be four men wearing balaclavas, left the scene in what is thought to be a dark coloured Vauxhall Vectra car.

Later on Saturday morning, at around 2.20am a second burglary took place at Mendlesham Community Centre on Old Station Road in Mendlesham. Suspect/s forced entry to the building and made untidy search, damaging some wooden shutters. It is not believed that anything was stolen.

It is reported that a man driving a truck was seen near the property around this time.

Officers are investigating possible links between these burglaries and similar incidents that took place in Norfolk at the weekend. Police are appealing for witnesses who may have seen or heard something suspicious around these times are asked to call 101 reference 53390/16 (Stanton) 53414/16 (Mendlesham) Alternatively contact Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111 or through their anonymous online form at www.crimestoppers-uk.org.

If you witnesses a crime in progress always call 999.
