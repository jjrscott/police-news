A 64-year-old man has been charged with a burglary which occurred in Trimley St Martin earlier this week.

The break-in occurred between 8:30am and 9:15am on Tuesday 30 January at a property in High Road.

At some point between the times stated, a patio door was forced and a quantity of cash and items of jewellery were stolen.

Philip Baker of Black Horse Lane, Ipswich, was arrested in connection with this incident yesterday morning, Thursday 1 January, after officers out on patrol had cause to stop a vehicle in Black Horse Lane.

He was taken to Martlesham Police Investigation Centre for questioning and has subsequently been charged with the following three offences: burglary; driving without insurance; and driving otherwise than in accordance with a licence.

Police are appealing for witnesses following a burglary at a home address in Trimley St Martin near Felixstowe.

The break-in occurred between 8:30am and 9:15am on Tuesday 30 January at a property in High Road.

At some point between the times stated, an offender forced open a patio door before carrying out an untidy search. A quantity of cash and items of jewellery were stolen.
