Additional images of stolen civic chains have been released by detectives investigating a theft at the Town Hall in Southwold.

The break-in took place sometime between Friday 2 and Monday 5 December at the address on Market Place.

The suspects forced entry via the rear of the building and made a search, before damaging a key cabinet and removing three civic chains – one large and two small.

Detectives have issued additional images of the mayoral chains stolen in an attempt to trace them and are appealing to anyone who recognises them, or who has information about the theft of the historic artefacts, to contact Lowestoft CID on **101** , quoting reference 69977/16. Alternatively, call Crimestoppers anonymously on **0800 555111**.

## Previous release - 5 December 2016

Officers are investigating the theft of several valuable civic chains, taken from the Town Hall in Southwold.

The theft took place sometime between Friday 2 and Monday 5 December 2016 at the address on Market Place.

The suspects forced entry via the rear of the building and made an untidy search, before damaging a key cabinet and removing three civic chains – one large and two small.

Anyone with information relating to this theft, or who knows of individuals trying to sell the historic artefacts, is asked to contact the Southwold Neighbourhood Support Team on **101** , quoting reference 69977/16. Alternatively, call Crimestoppers anonymously on **0800 555111**.
