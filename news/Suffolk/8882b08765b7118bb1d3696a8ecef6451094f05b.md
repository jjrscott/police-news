Officers are appealing for witnesses following a knifepoint robbery in Ipswich.

Earlier today, Sunday 1 October, at approximately 1.50am a man entered a business premises on Upper Brook Street in the town and threatened staff with a knife, demanding money. A sum of cash was handed over. The offender then threatened a customer and also stole cash from him.

Detectives investigating the incident now believe that the suspect cycled through the town centre immediately after committing the offence.

The suspect is described as a white man with a tanned complexion, tall and thin who was wearing a black jacket with the hood up, blue tracksuit bottoms with three white stripes down the side, black ankle high shoes with a white band around the sole, a green peaked cap and his face was partially hidden by a white face-covering.

Victims were very shaken as a result of the incident but nobody was injured.

Witnesses or anybody who has any information are asked to call Suffolk Police on **101** quoting reference 64287/17. Alternatively contact Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111 or through their anonymous online form at www.crimestoppers-uk.org.

## Previous release - 1 October

Officers are appealing for witnesses following a knifepoint robbery in Ipswich.

The suspect left the premises on foot in the direction of Buttermarket.

The offender is described as a white man with a tanned complexion, tall and thin who was wearing black clothes, a hooded top and had a balaclava covering his face.

Victims were very shaken as a result of the incident but nobody was injured.
