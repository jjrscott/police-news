Detectives investigating the fatal stabbing of Dean Stansby in Ipswich have arrested a further four men.

The arrests were all made this morning (Thursday 6 July) in London. They are a 23 year old man arrested on suspicion of murder, conspiracy to murder and being concerned in the supply of class A drugs.

A 24 year old man arrested for conspiracy to murder and being concerned in the supply of class A drugs. A 27 year old man arrested on suspicion for conspiracy to murder and being concerned in the supply of class A drugs and a 23 year old male arrested on suspicion for being concerned in the supply of class A drugs.

All four have been taken to Martlesham Police Investigation Centre for questioning.

41 year old Dean Stansby died after being stabbed on Wednesday 8 February in the vicinity of Ancaster Road in the town.

Four other people are currently on bail in connection with the incident:

A 36 year-old-man from Ipswich who was arrested on suspicion of assisting an offender has been re-bailed until 22 August
A 47-year-old man from Ipswich who was arrested on suspicion of murder has been re-bailed until 24 August
A 43 year-old-man of no fixed abode who was arrested on suspicion of murder has been re-bailed until 23 August
A 33-year-old man from Ipswich who was arrested on suspicion of murder has been re-bailed until 22 August
