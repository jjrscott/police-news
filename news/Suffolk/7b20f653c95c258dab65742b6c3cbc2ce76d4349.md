Officers investigating after a robbery at a business premises in Ipswich have today, Tuesday 16 October, arrested a man.

The robbery took place at around 9.00pm on Sunday 14 October, at the Ladbrokes betting shop on Nacton Road in the town.

Two male suspects entered the shop and one of them shouted at a male employee. The offender then grabbed the victim by his clothing and dragged him towards the counter before punching him in the head. The second offender, who was holding a knife, then stole a quantity of cash from the safe. Both offenders then fled the scene on foot.

The victim was not injured as a result of the incident but was left very shaken.

A 24-year-old man from Ipswich was arrested this morning on suspicion of robbery and has been brought to Martlesham Police Investigation Centre where he will be questioned by detectives.

Witnesses or anybody with any information are asked to call police on 101 quoting reference 59436/18. Alternatively contact the charity Crimestopppers anonymously on 0800 555 111.

## Previous release | 15 October

Officers are appealing for witnesses or information after a robbery at a business premises in Ipswich.

The robbery took place at around 9.00pm yesterday, Sunday 14 October, at the Ladbrokes betting shop on Nacton Road in the town.

Two male suspects entered the shop and one of them shouted at a male employee. The offender then grabbed the victim by his clothing and dragged him towards the counter before punching him in the head. The second offender, who was holding a knife, then stole a quantity of cash from the safe. Both offenders then fled the scene on foot.

The victim was not injured as a result of the incident but was left very shaken.

The first suspect is described as white, 6 feet tall, of slim build with short brown hair who was wearing a black hooded top. The second suspect is described as white, more than 6 feet tall, of slim athletic build who was carrying a green and white carrier bag.

Witnesses or anybody with any information are asked to call police on 101 quoting reference 59436/18. Alternatively contact the charity Crimestopppers anonymously on 0800 555 111.
