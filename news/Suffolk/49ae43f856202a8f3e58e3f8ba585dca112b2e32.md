A woman who police want to speak to in connection with an incident of ABH has been identified following a CCTV appeal.

A lady, aged in her late 80s was queuing in Marks and Spencer store on London Road in Lowestoft when she was caught by a suspect who was driving a mobility scooter.

The female pictured in the CCTV image has now been identified and will be spoken to by officers in due course.

## Previous release | 10 October 2017

Police are appealing for help to identify a woman in connection with an incident of actual bodily harm.

It happened on 1 September at some point between 11.30am and 12midday.

A lady, aged in her late 80s was queuing in Marks and Spencer store on London Road in Lowestoft.

She was caught by a suspect who was driving a mobility scooter causing the victim to fall to the floor and she also sustained a nasty cut to the leg in the process.

The driver of the scooter showed no remorse and also failed to apologise.

Officers would like to speak to the woman depicted in the CCTV still in connection with the incident.

Anyone who recognises the woman should contact PC Kevin Brooks at Lowestoft police on 101.
