A woman has been charged in connection with an incident of theft, possession of a Class B drug and for burglary offences in Lowestoft.

During the evening of Sunday 7 October, police received a call from a member of the public who reported witnessing an unknown woman acting suspiciously and entering several gardens.

Officers attended the area and found the woman in possession of a parcel which they suspected had been stolen. The woman was arrested on suspicion of burglary and taken to Great Yarmouth Police Investigation Centre for questioning.

Sharon Norman, 40, of no fixed address, was subsequently charged with burglary in connection with the incident on Sunday evening, when she stole a parcel, four keys on a fob and a small amount of cash from a property in Colville Road.

Norman was also charged in connection with two separate incidents. The first incident took place on Saturday 18 August and a second incident on Thursday 23 August. She was charged with an attempted burglary, theft and for possession of a Class B drug – amphetamines.

Norman was remanded into custody and was due to appear before Norwich Magistrates’ Court today, Tuesday 9 October.
