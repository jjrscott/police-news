Police have arrested four men on suspicion of hare coursing and causing criminal damage in Lavenham.

On Saturday 28 November at 11.50am, officers received reports from members of the public reporting suspicious activity in the Lavenham area.

A group of men in a vehicle were seen acting suspiciously with dogs on farmland and agricultural land was damaged by a vehicle.

Two 18-year-olds, a 20-year-old and a 21-year-old, all from the Essex area, were arrested on suspicion of hare coursing and criminal damage.

A vehicle and five dogs were seized by police.

The four men were taken to Bury St Edmunds police investigation centre, questioned and subsequently released under investigation pending further enquiries.

Officers would like to hear from anyone who may have seen any suspicious activity in the Lavenham or surrounding areas on Saturday 28 November.

Anyone with any information should contact Suffolk Police through the reporting page on the website quoting crime reference number 37/69042/20.
