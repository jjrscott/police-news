A 23-year-old man from Lowestoft has been charged following a robbery that took place at a convenience shop in the town on Wednesday 21 March.

Police were called at just before 10pm to reports of a man with a knife threatening staff at a SK News on Oxford Road. Officers were immediately despatched and on the scene in less than 5 minutes. A shop worker and members of the public intervened and restrained the man who was then arrested by police on suspicion of robbery and taken to Great Yarmouth Police Investigation centre where he was interviewed by detectives. Nobody was injured as a result of the incident.

Jack Burrows of Stoven Close, Lowestoft has been charged with robbery and possession of a bladed weapon in a public place.

Burrows is due to appear before Norwich Magistrates today, Friday 23 March.
