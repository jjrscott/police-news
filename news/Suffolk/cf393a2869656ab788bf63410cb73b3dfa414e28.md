Police investigating an allegation of robbery in Ipswich have arrested two people in connection with the incident following a CCTV appeal.

The offence took place at around 2.40am on Sunday 18 June at the junction of Norwich Road and Granville Street, where a man aged in his 30s was knocked to the ground and had a gold chain stolen.

Two men from Ipswich, aged 28 and 42, were arrested on suspicion of robbery in connection with the incident yesterday, Thursday 31 August.

They were taken to Martlesham Police Investigation for questioning and have both been released under investigation pending further enquiries.

## Previous release | 30 August 2017

Police are releasing CCTV images of two men officers would like to trace following a robbery in Ipswich.

On Sunday 18 June at around 02:40 am, a visually impaired man in his thirties was walking home when he was approached by two men at the junction of Norwich Road/Granville Street, who then assaulted him. The victim was knocked to the ground and a gold chain from around his neck was stolen. The offenders fled the scene, leaving the victim on the ground.

One of the offenders is described as in his twenties, with a tanned complexion, dark hair and a dark beard and was wearing a white T-shirt and dark trousers.

Police are keen to speak to the men pictured in connection with the incident.

Anyone with information about who either man is should call Suffolk Police on **101** , quoting crime number 42397/17. Alternatively contact Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111 or through their anonymous online form at www.crimestoppers-uk.org.
