Police are appealing for information after three burglaries at flats over public houses in the Suffolk Coastal area.

The first incident happened at a pub in the Felixstowe area between 7.30pm and 11.30pm on July 4 when someone gained entry to the premises by climbing onto a single storey roof. An iPad and a handbag were stolen.

The second happened at a pub on Westerfield Road in Westerfield between 10pm on July 7 and 8am on July 8, where a quantity of cash was stolen.

The third incident happened in Cumberland Street in Woodbridge around 11.20pm â€“ 11.30pm on July 16 where the offender gained access via a sloped roof and stole a quantity of cash. He was seen climbing down from the first floor of the premises before getting into a small white van that had been parked nearby.

The burglar is described as white, of small to medium build, around 5ft 8ins to 6ft tall with medium length brown hair.

Police are now releasing CCTV images in a bid to trace the man involved in this Woodbridge burglary (please see attached). Do you recognise him?

Anyone who may have information about these incidents or the person involved is asked to call South Suffolk CID by calling Suffolk Police on 101, quoting crime number 44873/16.

Police are also urging those managing public houses to review their security and to be vigilant for any suspicious activity. If you see anything unusual and suspect a crime may be in progress please dial 999 or if you would like advice about steps you can take to secure your premises you can call police on 101 for guidance or see our website here.
