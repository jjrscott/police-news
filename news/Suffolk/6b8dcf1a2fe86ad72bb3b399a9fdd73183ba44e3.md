A woman from Ipswich who died following a serious road traffic collision on the A14 at Copdock is being named by police.

Officers were called just before 9.30pm yesterday evening, Monday 7 January, to reports of a four-vehicle collision, initially occurring on the westbound on-slip and continuing into the carriageway.

The vehicles involved were a Hyundai i10, a Mercedes lorry, a Nissan Qashqai and a Peugeot 308.

Delcine Allen, aged 81 and who was driving the Hyundai, was subsequently taken to Ipswich Hospital in a serious condition, where she sadly later died.

The A14 westbound was closed between the Copdock and Sproughton junctions until 4am this morning while a collision investigation took place.

Any witnesses to this incident are asked to contact the Serious Collision Investigation Team on 101 quoting reference CAD 407 of 7 January.

## Previous release | 7 January 2019

Emergency services are currently at the scene of a serious road traffic collision on the A14 at Copdock.

Police were called just before 9.30pm this evening, Monday 7 January, to reports of a multiple vehicle collision on the westbound carriageway.

One woman has subsequently been taken to Ipswich Hospital in a serious condition.

The A14 westbound is currently closed between the Copdock and Sproughton junctions while a collision investigation takes place and this is expected to remain in place into the early hours of tomorrow morning.

Any witnesses to the collision are asked to contact the Serious Collision Investigation Team on 101 quoting reference CAD 407 of 7 January.
