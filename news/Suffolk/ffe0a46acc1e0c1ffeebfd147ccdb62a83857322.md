A 16-year-old boy has been charged with wounding with intent to cause grievous bodily harm following a stabbing in Ipswich on Monday 9 April.

Police were called at 7.55pm by the ambulance service following reports that a 17-year-old boy had been stabbed in Cemetery Road, Ipswich.

The boy had sustained a one inch stab wound in his back and was taken to Ipswich Hospital but was discharged the same night.

The 16-year-old boy was also charged with possession of bladed article and assault by beating.

## Previous release | 10 April 2018

A 16-year-old boy has been arrested on suspicion of malicious wounding with intent to cause grievous bodily harm (GBH) following a stabbing in Ipswich yesterday, Monday 9 April.

Police were called at 7:55pm by the Ambulance Service following reports that a 17-year-old boy had been stabbed by a suspect, who is believed to be known to him, in Cemetery Road, Ipswich.

The boy had sustained a one inch stab wound in his back and was taken to Ipswich Hospital for treatment but was discharged last night. A knife was also removed from the scene. The suspect is currently in custody at Martlesham Police Investigation Centre where he is awaiting questioning.

Anybody who witnessed the incident or who has any information is asked to call Suffolk police on 101, quoting reference 19119/18. Alternatively, call the charity Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111 or through their anonymous online form at www.crimestoppers-uk.org.
