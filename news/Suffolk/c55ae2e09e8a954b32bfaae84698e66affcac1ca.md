An 82-year-old woman who sadly died following a road collision in Stanstead has been formally named as Marina Medcalfe from the village.

Police were called at 3.15pm on Monday 26 June, to reports of a serious road traffic collision between a grey BMW car and a woman pedestrian on the B1066, Lower Street, Stanstead, near Long Melford .

The East of England Ambulance service and the East Anglian Air Ambulance also attended the scene, but the pedestrian Miss Medcalfe sadly died at the scene.

The family have asked us to issue the following tribute,

“82-year-old Marina had lived in the village of Stanstead all her life. She was greatly loved by all her nephews, nieces, great nephews and nieces, great great nephews and nieces and her many friends and neighbours.

“She was a staunch supporter of the church and local community and she really enjoyed her garden, opening it on occasions for village events.

“She will be sadly missed by all who knew her.”

 **Serious collision at Stanstead, near Long Melford**

Emergency services are currently at the scene of a serious road traffic collision in Stanstead, near Long Melford.

Police were called at 3.15pm to reports of a serious road traffic collision on the B1066 Lower Street, between a car and a pedestrian.

The road is currently closed in both directions and diversions are in place.
