An 18-year-old man arrested in connection with a reported rape in Newmarket has been bailed.

Police were contacted in the early hours of yesterday morning, Sunday 26 March, after a girl in her late teens reported that she been walking in the area known locally as Yellow Brick Road when she was approached by a man who had then raped her.

The man has been released on police bail and is due to return to Bury St Edmunds Police Investigation Centre on Tuesday 27 June.

It is believed that the 18-year-old man and the victim are known to each other and enquiries are ongoing.

Anyone with information regarding the incident are urged to contact Suffolk Police on **101** quoting CAD 53 of 26 March.

## Previous release | 26 March 2017

Police received a report in the early hours of this morning, Sunday 26 March, of a rape in Newmarket.

The victim is currently with specialist officers, and a scene remains in place at Fred Archer Way between Wellington Street and Exeter Road, known locally as Yellow Brick Road between Market Square and Exeter Road, while enquiries continue.

An 18-year-old man from Newmarket has been arrested in connection with the incident and is currently at Bury St Edmunds Police Investigation Centre, where he will be interviewed by officers.

Anyone with information regarding the incident is asked to contact Suffolk Police on 101 quoting CAD 53.
