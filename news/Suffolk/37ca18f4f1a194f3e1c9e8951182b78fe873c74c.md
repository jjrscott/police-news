Police are appealing for witnesses after a robbery in Glemsford, near Sudbury.

The incident took place at around 10:20pm on Friday 1 June in a property in Skates Hill.

A woman, aged her 20s, awoke to find a male offender, who was carrying a hammer, in her bedroom. He then grabbed her by the jaw and forced her to locate money and other items.
A second male, who was carrying a screwdriver, also threatened the woman.

A short time later, upon hearing someone arriving at the property, the offenders left the scene empty-handed and the woman was left uninjured.

The first suspect is described as white, aged between mid-20s to early 30s, 5ft 6in tall and with blue eyes. He was wearing black trousers, a black top with a hood up and a baseball cap. He was also wearing a black and white patterned scarf or bandana on his face and had a foreign accent.

The second suspect is described as white, aged between mid-20s to early 30s, 5ft 4in tall and with brown eyes. He was wearing black trousers, a matching black top and scarf or bandana covering his face. He also had a foreign accent.

Police would be interested in speaking to any witnesses who may have seen the suspects or any vehicles leaving the area of Skates Hill at around the time of the incident.

If you have any information which could help in the investigation contact Suffolk police on 101, quoting crime reference 37/30062/18 or call the charity Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555111.
