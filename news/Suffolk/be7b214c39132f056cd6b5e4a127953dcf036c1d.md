Police have issued an e-fit of a man they want to speak to in connection with a robbery in Ipswich.

The incident took place at some point between 7:45pm and 8pm on Sunday 4 February, when a 15-year-old boy, who was cycling in Princes Street was approached and shouted at to stop by an unknown man.

The man pulled out a knife before threatening the boy if he didn’t give him his bike, described as a bright orange, Carrera men’s mountain bike, while they were outside ZAK’s car wash, in the same street.

The boy dropped the bike to the floor and the man picked it up and cycled in the direction of Ipswich Railway Station.

Anyone with information relating to this crime, who recognises the man from the description or the man from the e-fit likeness ahould contact Ipswich CID on 101 quoting crime reference 37/6815/18 or alternatively contact the charity Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111.
