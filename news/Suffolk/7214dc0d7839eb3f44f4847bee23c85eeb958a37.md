Police investigating a knifepoint robbery at a business premises in Ipswich have arrested a man in connection with the incident.

The crime took place at around 6.50pm yesterday evening, Thursday 2 November, at a shop in Bramford Road (along the section between Chevallier Street and Wellington Street).

A man is reported to have entered the building and threatened a member of staff with a knife. He demanded that they open the till, from which he stole a quantity of bank notes. The offender then made off in the direction of Little Bramford Lane.

Officers have subsequently arrested a 34-year-old man from Ipswich on suspicion of robbery in connection with this incident. He has been taken to Martlesham Police Investigation Centre where he remains for questioning.

Detectives are appealing for any witnesses to this crime, or anyone who was in that area of Bramford Road between 6.30pm and 7pm and saw anyone acting suspiciously, to make contact.

Anyone with information about this incident is asked to call South CID on 101 quoting reference 71083/17, or Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111.
