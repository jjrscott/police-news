A man from Ipswich has been charged in connection with incidents in Reydon and Coddenham last week.
Police received a report of a post office burglary in Reydon, near Southwold on 25 October and an incident of making off without payment from a petrol station in Coddenham on 24 October.
A man was arrested on Friday 26 October and was taken to Martlesham police investigation centre for questioning by detectives.
Subsequently, 32-year-old Scott Hyam, of Ramsgate Drive in Ipswich, was charged with burglary and making off without payment.
He was remanded into custody and was due to appear at Ipswich Magistratesâ€™ Court this morning, Saturday 27 October at 10am.

## Previous Release | 25 October 2018

Police are appealing for witnesses following a number of break-ins across the county.

Overnight on Thursday 25 October, between midnight and 4.30am, a number of post offices and shops were broken into, including Shotley Post Office, stores at Reydon and Grundisburgh and an attempted burglary at Budgens in Great Blakenham.

An unknown quantity of items, including cigarettes and alcohol, were taken.

Officers are interested to hear from anyone who may have heard or seen any suspicious activity in these areas at the time of the offences.

Police are linking the incidents and would like to appeal to anyone who may have information to assist the investigation to contact Suffolk Police on 101, quoting crime reference number 37/61630/18.
