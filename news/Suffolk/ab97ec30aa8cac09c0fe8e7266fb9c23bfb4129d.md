Motorcycle and moped owners are being reminded to consider vehicle security after a number of vehicle thefts in Lowestoft.

At some point overnight between Monday 1 March at 10pm and Tuesday 2 March at 6am, a Honda Vision was stolen from an address on North Parade.

At some point between Thursday 4 March at 8.30pm and Friday 5 March at 3pm a black Honda Vision moped was taken from Compass Street.

On Friday 5 March between 1.30am and 9am, a Honda CBR 125 motorcycle was taken from Oulton Road and was later found damaged near the tram line on Gorse Green.

At some point between Friday 5 March at 11pm and Saturday 6 March at 7.25am, a black Honda SH 300 was taken from Grosvenor Road and was found damaged at a nearby location the following day.

At some point between Monday 1 March and Monday 8 March a moped was also stolen from Hollingsworth Road.

Police are linking these incidents and would encourage vehicle owners to be vigilant about security and to report any suspicious activity to police.

Motorcycle owners can find out more about deterring theft and improving motorcycle security through the First Principle section of the Suffolk Police website.

https://www.suffolk.police.uk/sites/suffolk/files/motorcyclesecurity.pdf

Anyone with any information about motorcycle and moped theft incidents or who saw anything suspicious at the time is asked to contact Suffolk Police quoting crime reference number 37/10471/21.

Website – http://www.suffolk.police.uk/contact-us/existing-report-update

Crimestoppers – Contact the independent charity Crimestoppers 100% anonymously on 0800 555 111, or via their online form: www.crimestoppers-uk.org

Phone – Call 101

Please note in the event of an emergency you should always call 999.
