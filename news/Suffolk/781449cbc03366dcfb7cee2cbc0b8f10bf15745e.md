Officers are appealing for witnesses or information following two burglaries that took place in Ipswich earlier this morning, Friday 26 August.

The first took place between 1.30am and 2.00am in Hening Avenue, Ipswich. Two offenders forced entry to the front of a business premises. The alarm was activated and the men fled the scene in a silver Volvo estate car.

It is believed that nothing was stolen. One of the men is described as wearing a dark hoodied top, jeans and had his face partially covered. The other man is thought to have been wearing red gloves.

The second incident happened at around 2.50 am in St Andrews Walk, Rushmere, Ipswich. Three offenders forced entry to a business premises and stole a quantity of spirits and cigarettes. The suspects are described as being three men who were all wearing black clothes.

Police are investigating possible links between these two incidents.

Witnesses or anybody who has any information are asked to call Suffolk Police on **101** , reference 51923/16 (Hening Avenue) or 51916/16 (St Andrews Place) Alternatively, contact Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111 or through their anonymous online form at http://www.crimestoppers-uk.org/.
