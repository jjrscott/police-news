Police can confirm that a man has died following a serious collision in Lavenham last week.

Officers were called at 11.30am on Monday 25 June to reports of a collision on the A1141 Bury Road, to the north of Lavenham, which involved a grey Jaguar F-Type and a blue BMW 740LE car.

The driver of the Jaguar car, a man in his 70s, sustained serious injuries and was taken by air ambulance to Addenbrooke’s Hospital. The man has now sadly died in hospital.

Witnesses or anybody with any information regarding the collision are asked to call the Serious Collision Investigation Team on 101 quoting reference CAD 145 of 25 June.

## Previous release | 25 June

Emergency services are currently on the scene of a serious road traffic collision on the A1141, on the outskirts of Lavenham.

Police were called at 11.30am today, Monday 25 June to reports of a collision involving a grey Jaguar F-Type and a blue BMW 740LE car. The driver of the Jaguar car, a man, sustained serious injuries and has been taken by air ambulance to Addenbrooke’s Hospital.

The A1141 between Lavenham and Thorpe Morieux is closed at this time to allow an investigation into the circumstances to take place.

Witnesses or anybody with any information regarding the collision are asked to call the Serious Collision Investigation Team on 101 quoting reference CAD 145 of today.
