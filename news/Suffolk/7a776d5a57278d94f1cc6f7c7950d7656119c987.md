Police are appealing for help to trace the owners of a dog following an incident in Oulton Broad.

The incident occurred on Friday 7 June, at around 5pm, when a woman was walking through Bonds Meadow Nature Reserve and a small grey French bulldog, which was wearing a harness, a lead and being restrained by a male and female, managed to bite the woman on her calf, drawing blood. The male and female apologised and then walked off.

Anyone who witnessed the incident or anyone with information is asked to visit www.suffolk.police.uk/contact-us/existing-report-update quoting crime reference 37/34334/19, or telephone 101 or contact A/PS Allard by emailing [email protected]

Alternatively, contact Crimestoppers 100% anonymously on 0800 555 111, or via their online form: www.crimestoppers-uk.org
