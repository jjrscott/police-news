A 21-year-old man has been charged after a takeaway delivery driver was robbed in Haverhill.

The incident occurred at around 7.20pm on Wednesday 29 June in Shepherds Court.

The victim – a woman aged in her forties – was delivering food to an address in Shepherds Court, when she was assaulted and then had a shoulder bag containing cash and bank cards stolen.

The victim was taken to Addenbrooke’s Hospital having sustained head injuries, but is in a stable condition.

Hari Farlie, of Montfort Court, Haverhill, was arrested in connection with this incident and taken to Bury St Edmunds Police Investigation Centre for questioning and has subsequently been charged with robbery.

Farlie was also charged with three further offences:

 * One count of assault causing actual bodily harm; and one count of assault by beating; following an incident in Fornham Road, Bury St Edmunds on Saturday 20 February 2016
 * One count of assault causing actual bodily harm following an incident in Feltham, west London, on Friday 20 November 2015

He has been remanded in custody and will appear before Ipswich Magistrates’ Court this morning, Friday 1 July.
