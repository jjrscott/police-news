Two men were charged yesterday, Thursday 10 May, following the discovery of a cannabis factory in Lowestoft.

Indrit Beshiri and Ndriqim Teta, both 26 and of no fixed abode, have been charged with the production of cannabis and for abstracting electricity.

Officers from the East Scorpion team attended an address in Kirkley Run and discovered 162 cannabis plants growing across the area of three separate rooms in a property on Wednesday 9 May. Electricity was also being abstracted.

The two men were arrested on Wednesday 9 May, and were taken to Great Yarmouth Police Investigation Centre for questioning.

Beshiri and Teta were remanded and are due to appear at Norwich Magistratesâ€™ Court today.

## Previous release | 10 May 2018

Two men have been arrested on suspicion of cannabis production after police uncovered a cannabis factory at an address in Lowestoft yesterday, Wednesday 9 May.

Officers from the East Scorpion Team attended an address in Kirkley Run and discovered approximately 200 cannabis plants growing in three rooms of a property. Electricity was also being abstracted.

Two 26-year-old men, both of no fixed abode, were subsequently arrested on suspicion of the production of cannabis and for abstracting electricity. They were taken to Great Yarmouth Police Investigation Centre for questioning where they currently remain.
