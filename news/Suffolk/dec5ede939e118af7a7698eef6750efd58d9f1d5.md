A 70-year-old motorcyclist has died following a serious road traffic collision in Flixton last month.

Officers were called at around 3.15pm on Saturday 22 September to reports of a single-vehicle incident in The Street (B1062), involving a black Triumph Bonneville motorcycle which had left the road and collided with a tree.

Michael McQuat, from Lowestoft, was taken to Addenbrooke’s Hospital by Air Ambulance with life-threatening injuries, but has sadly died.

Any witnesses, or anyone who saw a motorcycle matching the description above immediately prior to the collision, are asked to contact the Serious Collision Investigation Team on 101 quoting reference CAD 233 of 22 September.

## Previous release | 24 September 2018

Police are appealing for witnesses following a serious road traffic collision in Flixton at the weekend.

Officers were called at around 3.15pm on Saturday 22 September to reports of a single-vehicle incident in The Street (B1062), involving a black Triumph Bonneville motorcycle which had left the road and collided with a tree.

The rider – a man aged in his 70s – was taken to Addenbrooke’s Hospital by Air Ambulance having sustained life-threatening injuries. The road was closed until 7.30pm whilst a collision investigation took place.

Any witnesses, or anyone who saw a motorcycle matching the description above immediately prior to the collision, are asked to contact the Roads and Armed Policing Team on 101 quoting reference CAD 233 of 22 September.
