Police are appealing for witnesses following three burglaries in west Suffolk villages, all occurring on Wednesday 4 January.

The first incident occurred between 8.45am and 11.30am at an address in Rushbrooke. A window was forced open to gain entry to the property and a quantity of jewellery and an iPad were stolen.

The second break-in took place between 9.30am and 6pm, at a property on Felsham Road in Bradfield St George. A rear window was forced open to gain entry and items of jewellery and an iPad were stolen.

The final burglary occurred between 12.30pm and 10.15pm at a property in Mill Road, Buxhall, where an attempt was made to force open the front door, before entry was gained via the back door and jewellery stolen from inside.

Detectives are appealing for anyone who may have seen any suspicious persons or vehicles in any of the locations above on Wednesday to contact them.

Anyone with any information about these burglaries is asked to contact Bury St Edmunds CID on **101** quoting references: 10588/17 for Rushbrooke; 10638/17 for Bradfield St George; or 10653/17 for Buxhall; or call Crimestoppers anonymously on **0800 555 111**.
