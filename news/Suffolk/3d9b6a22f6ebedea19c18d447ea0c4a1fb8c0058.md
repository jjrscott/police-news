A seventh person has been arrested after a teenage boy was assaulted in Ipswich last week.

Police were called at around 3.50pm on Wednesday 17 May to reports of a fight in Moffat Avenue. On arrival, officers found a 16-year-old boy had sustained a puncture wound. He was taken to Ipswich Hospital where he was treated for his injuries.

Later the same afternoon, police arrested four teenage boys and two teenage girls (aged between 15 and 17) on suspicion of affray in connection with this incident. They were all released under investigation after being interviewed at Martlesham Police Investigation Centre (PIC).

On Friday 19 May officers investigating this assault arrested an 18-year-old man from Ipswich on suspicion of assault causing grievous bodily harm and affray. He was taken to Martlesham PIC for questioning and has also been released under investigation pending further enquires.

Detectives are continuing to appeal for any witnesses to this incident to come forward. Anyone with information is asked to contact South CID at Landmark House on 101 quoting reference 35699/17, or Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111.
