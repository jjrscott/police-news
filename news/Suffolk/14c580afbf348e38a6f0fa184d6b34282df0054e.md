Police are appealing for help to identify a man following a sexual assault in Ipswich.

Officers are today issuing CCTV pictures of a man that they would like to trace in connection with the incident which took place sometime between 3am and 4am on Saturday 1 July in the town.

The woman, in her teens, had been walking alone along Falcon Street when she was approached by a man asking for directions. He continued trying to engage in conversation as she walked in the direction of The Corn Exhange and Crown Street and is reported to have attempted to kiss her and put his hands inside her clothing.

The victim pushed him away and managed to get him to leave.

The suspect is described as around 18 – 25 years’ old, approximately 6ft in height and of slim build. He has dark hair, which is shaved at the sides, and was wearing black jeans, a dark bomber-style jacket and trainers.

Police would like to speak to the man pictured in connection with this incident and are appealing for him, or anyone with information about him, to contact Suffolk Police on 101.

Anyone with any information, or who may have been in the area at the time is asked to contact Suffolk Police on 101 quoting 45464 /17. Alternatively, contact Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555111 or through their anonymous online form at www.crimestoppers-uk.org.
