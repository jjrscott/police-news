A 25-year-old Ipswich man has been charged with attempted grievous bodily harm, possession of a knife in a public place and assaulting a police officer after an incident in Ipswich.

Police were called on Friday 18 July at 8:45pm to reports of a man holding a knife, chasing another man in Dickens Road in the town. Police were on the scene quickly and arrested a 25-year-old man on suspicion of affray. He was brought to Martlesham Police Investigation Centre for questioning.

Luke Mason of Old Norwich Road, Ipswich has now been charged with attempted grievous bodily harm, intentionally threatening a person whilst in possession of a knife in a public place, and assaulting an emergency worker.

Mason appeared before Ipswich Magistrates Court on Monday 20 July and has been remanded into custody.
