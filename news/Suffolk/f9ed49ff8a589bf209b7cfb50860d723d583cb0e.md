Five men remain on police bail following an incident in Foundation Street in Ipswich at the end of last year, whilst six other people have had their bail cancelled.

Police were contacted by the Ambulance Service at 10pm on Sunday 18 December, following reports of two seriously injured men on Foundation Street. The men were taken to hospital in a critical condition, but both have since recovered.

As detectives continue their enquiries into this incident, five people remain on police bail as follows:

 * A 24-year-old man from Ipswich, who was arrested on suspicion of attempted murder late on Monday 19 December. He has been re-bailed to return to police on Thursday 25 May.
 * Two men from Luton, both aged 20, who were arrested on suspicion of attempted murder and conspiracy to supply controlled drugs on Thursday 22 December. They have been re-bailed to return to police on Wednesday 24 May.
 * Two men from Felixstowe, aged 30-years-old and 26-years-old, both arrested on Wednesday 11 January, on suspicion of violent disorder, possession of a controlled drug and possession of a bladed article in a public place. They have been re-bailed until Wednesday 24 May and Thursday 25 May respectively.

Six other people arrested in connection with this incident will face no further police action at this time.

Officers are still keen to hear from anyone who was in the vicinity of Blackfriars Court, Foundation Street, Lower Orwell Street or Rosemary Lane in Ipswich shortly before 10pm on Sunday 18th December.

Any witnesses or anyone with information about this incident is asked to contact Suffolk Police on 101 quoting crime number 72333/16. Alternatively contact Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111.

## Foundation Street area - serious assault

Suffolk Police have established a police cordon in Ipswich town centre following reports of a serious assault.

Police were contacted by the East of England Ambulance Service at 10pm on Sunday 18 December, following reports of two seriously injured men on Foundation Street in Ipswich.

Both men have been taken to hospital in a critical condition, with what are being described as life threatening injuries.

Two people have been detained by police in connection with the incident and will be questioned by detectives.

Foundation Street will be closed between Wingfield Street and Smart Street, and Rosemary Lane and the NCP Blackfriars car park will also be closed, while investigations continue.

The road and car park closures are expected to remain in place at least for the duration of Monday morning (19 December).

Anyone with information about this incident or who may have seen supicious activity in the area is asked to contact Suffolk Police on 101 quoting CAD number 391 of Sunday 18 December, or alternatively contact Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111.
