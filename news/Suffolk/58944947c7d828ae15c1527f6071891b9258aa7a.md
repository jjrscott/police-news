Two men have been charged with drugs offences after being arrested at Normanston Drive in Lowestoft yesterday.

Plain clothes officers from the East Scorpion Team, based in the town, noticed suspicious activity in the area yesterday afternoon, Monday 19 June, and their uniformed colleagues went to a nearby home where the two men - a 26-year-old and a 19-year-old - were arrested.

Saed Tarabi, 26, of Park Terrace, East Kilbride, Glasgow, and Dondrea Thomas, 19, formerly of Buttmarsh Close, south east London, have both now been charged with being concerned in the supply of crack cocaine.

Both are being detained in custody pending an appearance before Norwich Magistrates tomorrow morning, Wednesday 21 June.

## Previous release | 20 June 2017

Two men have been arrested on suspicion of class A drugs offences after police went to an address in Normanston Drive in Lowestoft yesterday.

Plain clothes officers from the East Scorpion Team, based in the town, noticed suspicious activity in the area yesterday afternoon, Monday 19 June, and their uniformed colleagues went to a nearby home where the two men - a 26-year-old and a 19-year-old - were detained.

The 26-year-old, from East Kilbride, Glasgow, was arrested on suspicion of possession of a class A drugs with intent to supply, while the 19-year-old, from south-east London, was arrested for being concerned in the supply of class A drugs.

Several mobile phones and a large quantity of cash were seized at the property.

Both men are currently in custody where they are being interviewed in connection with drugs offences.
