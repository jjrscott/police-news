Two men have been charged in connection with an incident of affray in Lowestoft on Sunday evening.

Police were called shortly after 9.40pm on Sunday 22 July to reports that a female had been threatened by two men, one of whom was alleged to be in possession of a knife, outside the Royal Dragon takeaway in St Peter's Street.

On arrival, officers quickly dispersed an altercation and a knife was recovered from the scene. Two men were also arrested and taken to Great Yarmouth Police Investigation Centre for questioning.

Andrew Ball, 24, of Old Market Plain, Lowestoft, was subsequently charged with being in possession of an offensive weapon in a public place. He was remanded and was due to appear before Norwich Magistratesâ€™ Court today, Tuesday 24 July.

Luke Fairman, 24, of Cowslip Crescent, Lowestoft, was subsequently charged with using threatening words and behaviour with intent to cause fear. Fairman has been bailed to appear before Great Yarmouth Magistrates' Court on Wednesday 22 August.
