A Cleethorpes man who repeatedly breached an order put in place to protect his family from domestic abuse has been given a 1 year suspended jail sentence.

Bailey Walsh, 22, of Issac’s Hill, admitted that although he is banned from going to the home of one his family members, he had turned up on three occasions.

On Tuesday, 22 December; Christmas Day and on Sunday, 27 December, he went to the property and repeatedly pressed the intercom, asking for money, food and cigarettes.

He also telephoned and tried to make contact via social media and was arrested after being located by officers in Clee Road, Cleethorpes, as part of Operation Galaxy.

Grimsby Magistrates Court also imposed a 12 month rehabilitation order and added to the restrictions of his restraining order, which will remain in place until January 2023 and he was ordered to pay £85 costs and a £128 with £85 costs.

If you are a victim of domestic abuse or you’re worried about a friend or family member, please get in touch so that we can help you.

Call us on 101 or, if you or someone else is in immediate danger, call 999.
