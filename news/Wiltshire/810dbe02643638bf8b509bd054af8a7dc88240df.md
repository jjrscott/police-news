A Salisbury man has been convicted and sentenced for two counts of drink driving within the space of six weeks.

Jake Quinn, 23, of Maryland Close, Salisbury, has been disqualified from driving for 3 years, ordered to pay a fine of £966, a victim surcharge of £86 and costs of £170.

On the night of 17 October 2020, Quinn was stopped on London Road, Amesbury, after officers saw him driving in the dark with no lights on.

And on the evening of 27 November 2020, he was seen driving erratically in Salisbury by police, and eventually stopped on Maryland Close, Salisbury.

On both occasions Quinn provided samples of breath over the legal limit.

He was sentenced at Salisbury Magistrates Court on yesterday (04/02)
