_Date published: 10 December 2020 11:35_

 _Dated: 10 December 2020 11:33:34_

Eight people have been charged following a racially aggravated assault in Sheffield city centre this summer.

At around 6pm on 13 June, it was reported that a group of people were walking past Yates on Cambridge Street, when they were confronted by another group. The altercation led to a 55-year-old man and a 20-year-old woman being assaulted, suffering minor injuries.

Eight people from Sheffield have now been charged in connection with the incident, their details are listed in full below:

Steven Carrol, 28, of Maltravers Terrace: charged with affray and common assault
Wayne Garrity, 31, of Derby Street: charged with affray and racially aggravated common assault
David Hill, 27, of Skelton Grove: charged with affray
Luke Hodder, 28, of Burnaby Street: charged with affray
Iain Inkson, 56, of Stradbroke Road: charged with affray
Hayley Holdsworth, 19, of Maltravers Crescent: charged with affray
Brandon Moxon, 22, of Maltravers Crescent: charged with affray
Michael Storey, 57, of Skelton Lane: charged with affray

A ninth person, a 16-year-old boy, has been reported on summons for affray and racially aggravated common assault.

They have all been bailed and will appear at court on dates between April and May 2021.
