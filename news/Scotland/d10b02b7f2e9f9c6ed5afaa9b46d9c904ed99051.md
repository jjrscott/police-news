**Published 10th June 2020**

Police in Dumbarton are continuing enquiries and appealing for information following an assault on a 33-year-old man in Dumbarton on Saturday, 6 June 2020.

Around 11.50 pm on Saturday, 6 June, the 33-year-old man was attacked in Ardoch Crescent by a group of men brandishing a variety of weapons.

The men then made off in two cars in the direction of Glencairn Road, Dumbarton.
The 33-year-old man was taken by ambulance to the Royal Alexandra Hospital in Paisley where he was treated for his injuries.

Area Commander Chief Inspector Scott Carlin, said:

“Violence and antisocial behaviour will not be tolerated and will be dealt with robustly. We have additional officers on patrol in the area and I would encourage anyone with concerns to speak to them.

“It is important that we trace those responsible and colleagues in CID are following a positive line of enquiry however I would urge anyone with any information that may assist this enquiry to contact police.”

Anyone with any information is asked to contact police via 101, quoting incident number 4691 of Saturday 6 June 2020. Alternatively you can contact Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111 where information can be given in confidence.
