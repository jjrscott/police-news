Detectives are investigating a housebreaking at a church in the Wyndford area of Glasgow which occurred on Tuesday 6 October 2020.

Around 2.45pm a priest disturbed two male youths who had broken into St Gregory’s Church in Kelvindale Street, and vandalised property within.

The first suspect is described as white, around 12 to 13 years old, 5ft4, of slim build with short dark hair. He was wearing black trousers, a white shirt and had a black backpack.

The second suspect is described as white, 4ft8 and was wearing a black hoody with the hood up and black trousers.

They ran off in the direction of the Wyndford estate.

Detective Constable Peter Lagan from Maryhill CID said: “I am appealing for anyone who was near to St Gregory’s Church yesterday afternoon, and either saw two male youths breaking in or running away, to please get in touch.

“I would also urge any motorists with dash-cams who were in the vicinity to check their footage in case they have captured anything which could be of significance.

“Anyone with information is asked to contact Police Scotland via 101, quoting incident number 1866 of Tuesday 6 October 2020. Alternatively you can call Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111, where details can be given anonymously.”
