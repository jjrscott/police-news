* Belfast

Robbery - Stranmillis Road, Belfast

Detectives are appealing for information following the report of a robbery at a restaurant in the Stranmillis Road area of Belfast last night, Wednesday, 29 June.

Shortly after 9.30pm Police received a report that three men, one of whom was armed with what is believed to be a firearm, entered the premises and stole money from a till.

A description of the alleged perpetrators is as follows:-

Male 1 – Approximately 170 cms tall, thin build, approximately 20 years old. He was wearing a dark coloured pullover jumper.

Male 2 – Approximately 185 cms tall and medium build. He was wearing a dark jumper.

Male 3 – Approximately 170cm tall and aged in his late teens. He was wearing a dark blue jacket with a dark hood and light grey coloured sports trousers.

All three men left the scene on foot.

Detective Sergeant Keith Wilson would appeal to anyone who has any information about this incident to contact Detectives at Reactive and Organised Crime at Musgrave on the non-emergency number 101. Altneratively, if someone would prefer to provide information without giving their details they can contact the independent charity Crimestoppers and speak to them anonymously on 0800 555 111.
