Thames Valley Police is appealing for witnesses following a robbery in Oxford.

At around 11pm on Monday (11/1) the victim, a 28-year-old man, was walking through Sandy Lane Park near to the junction of Balfour Road when he was hit from behind and repeatedly kicked whilst on the floor by a group of five unknown men.

The victim's mobile phone was stolen.

The victim sustained bruising to his body and head, but did not require hospital treatment.

One of the offenders is described as a white man, around 6ft tall with short dark hair and in his early twenties. He was wearing a coloured jacket and jeans.

Designated Investigator Marie Doherty based at Oxford police station, said: "This was a frightening experience for the victim.

"I would like to appeal for any witnesses who may have been in the area at the time, or who may have relevant CCTV or dash cam footage to come forward. You can contact us using our online form or by calling 101, quoting reference number 43210014558.

"Alternatively, you can call Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111 or make a report online."
