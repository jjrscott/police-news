Thames Valley Police is appealing for witnesses following a GBH incident which occurred in Bracknell.

The incident took place on Tuesday (1/12) between 2.30pm and 2.45pm in Garth Meadows, Harvest Ride.

The victim, a 23-year-old man, was assaulted by two men he did not know. He sustained three fractured ribs and a punctured lung.

He was taken to hospital for treatment but has since been discharged.

The first offender was wearing a dark jumper and had a tattoo of a rose on his hand.

The second offender was about 6ft tall with a muscular build, curly black hair and brown eyes. He was wearing a black mask covering his nose and mouth, a black Nike jumper, black trousers and had a tattoo on the side of his neck.

Investigating officer, PC James Clifton, based at Loddon Valley police station, said: "I would like to appeal to anyone who witnessed this incident to come forward.

"If you have any details which you think could be relevant to our investigation, please let us know.

"Please call the Thames Valley Police non-emergency number on 101, quoting reference number 43200404019, or contact Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111. Alternatively, you can make a report online."
