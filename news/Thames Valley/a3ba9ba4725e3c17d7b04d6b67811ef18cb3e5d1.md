Thames Valley Police is releasing CCTV images of a man officers would like to speak to in connection with an investigation into a robbery, which occurred in Bletchley.

At around 10pm on 4 September 2020, the victim, a man in his fifties, was walking through the shops area of Serpentine Court when he was approached by a group of youths.

One of the offenders held a knife to the victim and demanded his property. The victim was then assaulted.

He was knocked unconscious and sustained injuries to his face. He was taken to hospital for treatment and has since been discharged.

The victim's bag and phone were stolen.

Investigating officer, Detective Constable Jo Hudson, based at Milton Keynes police station, said: "We are releasing these images as we believe the individual pictured may have important information about this investigation.

"If you recognise who this is, or if you think it may be you, please get in touch.

"Anyone with information can contact police by calling 101 or making a report online, quoting reference 43200277624.

"Alternatively, if you wish to remain anonymous, you can contact the independent charity Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111."
