Thames Valley Police is appealing for witnesses following an incident in which a man exposed himself in Slough.

Between 4.40pm and 5.05pm on Tuesday (16/3) a man was seen touching himself inappropriately under his trousers while the victims, two women in their twenties, were walking past him in Lascelles Park. He followed the victims along Lascelles Road while continuing to touch himself over his clothing.

They walked past the traffic lights in Sussex Place before he then exposed himself to one of the women. He left in the direction of Langley.

The offender is described as mixed race, around 5ft 7ins tall, slim, and aged in his early twenties. He has black hair and was wearing a blue tracksuit jacket with tracksuit trousers.

Investigating officer PC Siobhan Raine, of the Incident and Crime Response Team based at Slough police station, said: "I would like to appeal to anyone who witnessed the incident, or who believes they saw the offender at around the time of the incident. If you have any information, or have any dash-cam footage or CCTV which may assist the investigation, please come forward and speak to police."

Anyone with information should contact the 24-hour non-emergency number 101 or make a report online, quoting the reference 43210110482.

Alternatively you can contact the independent charity Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111.
