Thames Valley Police is appealing for witnesses following an incident of grievous bodily harm in Maidenhead.

At around 9pm on Sunday (21/2) there was an altercation between two men in Ray Mead Road.

One man, aged in his forties, sustained serious swelling and bruising to his body as well as facial fractures. His injuries required hospital treatment, however he has since been discharged.

A 46-year-old man from Maidenhead was arrested on suspicion of section 18 grievous bodily harm with intent and has been released on police bail.

Investigating officer, Detective Constable William Holbrook, based at Maidenhead police station, said: "We are appealing for any witnesses to this incident, or anyone who might have information about what happened, to please come forward.

"We believe that this was an isolated incident and that both men were known to each other.

"Anyone with information can contact police by calling 101 or making a report online, quoting reference 43210073833.

"Alternatively, if you wish to remain anonymous, you can contact the independent charity Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111 or online."
