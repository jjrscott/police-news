Thames Valley Police is appealing for witnesses after two teenagers were robbed in Oxford.

Between 6pm and 6.20pm yesterday (6/2) the two teenagers, both aged 18, were approached by a man in Broad Oak Park, adjacent to the Eastern Bypass Road in Wood Farm, Oxford.

The offender threatened the victims with a knife making a stabbing moving towards one of them before stealing a bag.

The victims managed to push the suspect and ran away uninjured.

The offender is described as a white man, aged in his early twenties with a slim build.

He was between 5ft 11ins and 6ft 3ins tall and of a slim build, wearing dark clothing, similar to tracksuit bottoms and top with a hood up.

The bag that was stolen was a black Adidas shoulder bag with three silver horizontal stripes (pictured).

Investigating officer Detective Constable Archie Coyne of the Priority Crime Team based at Oxford police station said: "I am appealing to anybody that was in the general area of Broad Oak Park, Wood Farm, yesterday between 6pm and 6.20m who believes that they witnessed this incident to please contact police.

"Thankfully, the victims were not injured in this ordeal, but property was stolen and the offender made off on foot after they pushed him away.

"Anybody with any information should call the 24-hour non-emergency number 101 quoting reference number 43210052011 or alternatively make a report online.

"You can also call the independent charity Crimestoppers 100% anonymously on 0800 555 111."
