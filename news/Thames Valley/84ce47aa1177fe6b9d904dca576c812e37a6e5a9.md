Thames Valley Police is appealing for witnesses following an incident of wounding with intent that took place in Long Hanborough, near Witney in West Oxfordshire.

At 9.15pm on Sunday (7/2), a man aged in his thirties was assaulted in Main Road, Long Hanborough.

The incident took place at the junction with Regent Drive. The victim sustained injuries including a broken arm, lacerations and bruising. He was taken to hospital where he remains in a stable condition.

A 22-year-old man from Witney, and three other men aged 18, 25 and 26, all from Oxford, have been arrested on suspicion of wounding with intent to cause grievous bodily harm and remain in police custody.

Detective Sergeant Rob Platt, of Banbury Police station, said: "This was a particularly violent incident, during which the victim has sustained injuries requiring hospital admission.

"Officers have made four arrests in connection with this incident, however we are appealing for anyone in the local area that might have information to come forward.

"Anyone that can help is asked to call Thames Valley Police on 101, quoting reference 43210053198.

"You can also make a report online, or call Crimestoppers for 100% anonymity on 0800 555 111."
