Police are appealing for witnesses following a burglary at The Newell Centre in Tozer Way, Chichester.

A sound system consisting of speaker and microphone, worth approximately Â£500, is reported to have been stolen between 8pm on Tuesday 13 October and 7.30am on Wednesday 14 October.

Around 7pm on Monday 12 October, a man and a woman were seen to enter The Newell Centre and act suspiciously, before leaving.

The man is described as 5'4", mid-late 30s, skinny, with a gaunt face and unshaven, wearing a denim baseball cap, a black zipped bomber jacket, a red-and-white scarf and skinny fit blue jeans.

The woman is described as 5'4", mid-late 30s, with blonde mousey hair in a ponytail, a rounded face with of a slight tan, and wearing a leopard print face mask with a blue denim jacket, white T-shirt and skinny fit blue denim jeans.

Police are keen to hear from anyone who may have seen a man and woman matching this description around that time, or indeed anyone seen acting suspiciously around the time of the burglary.

Specifically, officers would like to establish the identity of the woman wearing the face mask, who was accompanying the man.

Anyone with any information is asked to report it online or call 101, quoting serial 174 of 14/10.
