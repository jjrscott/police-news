Police investigating a burglary in Ferring, near Angmering, are appealing for help in tracing a significant amount of property stolen, including a transit van.

The incident took place on Sunday, 6 December 2020 at Roundstone Farm in Littlehampton Road, Ferring, between 1am and 6.30am.

Two units were broken into and a significant amount of gardening, landscaping and construction equipment was stolen. The stolen transit van is white with red and green signage.

Police reviewed CCTV footage and two people were seen at the location. Both were wearing balaclavas and gloves.

Detective Constable Jordan Holmes said: "We are keen to hear from anyone who may have seen or been offered these stolen goods or who has any information which can further assist our investigation.

"We have been conducting lines of enquiry into this investigation, which has resulted in the arrest of a 34-year-old man from Worthing on suspicion of burglary.

"He was also further arrested on suspicion of possession of small quantities of class A and class B drugs. The man has been released under investigation."

Anyone with any information can contact Sussex Police by reporting online or calling 101 and quoting 0249 of 06/12.

Alternatively, you can visit the CrimeStoppers website or contact the independent charity anonymously on 0800 555 111.
