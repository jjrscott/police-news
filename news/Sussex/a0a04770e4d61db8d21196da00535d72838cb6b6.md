Police would like to speak to this man in relation to a sexual assault in Worthing town centre.

Between 00:12am and 1am on Tuesday (July 28), a man has followed a woman along Warwick Street, South Street, Marine Parade, Montague Place, Montague Street and back into South Street.

During this time, the victim has reported two sexual assaults taking place in Warwick Street and Montague Street.

Officers investigating the matter are keen to speak to anyone who witnessed either assault, or who saw any suspicious behaviour in the area at the time.

They are particularly keen to speak to the man pictured, as they believe he can help with their enquiries.

He is described as black, in his 30s, between 5' 2" and 5' 6" tall, and with short, black, dreadlock style hair that's approximately one inch long. He was wearing white shoes, black jeans and a black hooded jumper with a large white logo on the front. He had no facial hair.

Anyone who recognises him, or who has any other information relating to the incident, is asked to contact police online or by calling 101 quoting serial 59 of 28/07.

Alternatively, you can contact the independent charity Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111.
