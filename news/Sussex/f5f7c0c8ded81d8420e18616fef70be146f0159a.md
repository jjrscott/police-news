Updated News Post

A man has been arrested and charged with arson after a Pride flag was set on fire in Brighton, on Sunday (2 August).

Moulay Abdelhakim Ismaili, 36, of Beaconsfield Road, Brighton, was detained on suspicion of committing arson after after a Pride flag was taken from a garden in Beaconsfield Road, and the flag stick was set on fire and pushed through a nearby letterbox of a property at about 9.55pm. Fortunately no one was hurt.

Ismaili was remanded in custody to appear at Brighton Magistrates' Court on Tuesday (4 August).

Sussex Police are committed to fully investigating reports of hate crime. If you have been a victim of or have witnessed a hate crime, we urge you to either report online, call us on 101, or 999 if it is an emergency.
