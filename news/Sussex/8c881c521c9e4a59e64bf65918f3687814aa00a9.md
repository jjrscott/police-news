Police made a further arrest in connection with a stabbing following a fight in Montpelier Place at 10.15pm on Wednesday, 4 November.

A 40-year-old local man suffered stab injuries and was taken to Royal Sussex County Hospital for treatment. He has since been discharged.

A 19-year-old local man was arrested on suspicion of attempted murder at a property in Hove at 8am on Thursday, 25 March, and has been released under investigation, pending further enquiries.

Six other men, all arrested on suspicion of attempted murder, have been released under investigation, pending further enquiries. They are four 18-year-olds, a 19-year-old and a 57-year-old, all from Brighton and Hove.

Detective Inspector James Ansell, of the Surrey and Sussex Police Major Crime Team, said: "This further arrest represents positive developments in this investigation we continue to appeal for information from the public.

"We are confident that someone knows what happened and has the information we need as we work to establish the full circumstances. We urge them to do the right thing and speak to us.

"This incident happened in a busy area of Brighton and we continue to appeal for anyone with any information - including any relevant CCTV, dash cam or mobile phone footage - to contact us.

"The victim and suspects are believed to be known to each other and this is being treated as an isolated incident with no threat to the wider public."

You can report information either online or by calling 101, quoting Operation Moorcroft.

You can also contact the independent charity Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111 or report it online. www.crimestoppers-uk.org
