Some of our cases during March 2021 which have led to charges and/or convictions in court.

Setencings during the month have included a woman who was found guilty of the murder of her partner following a re-trial at court after an appeal against the previous conviction was made, a man sentenced to more than 12 years imprisonment following a stabbing and for class A drugs offences and a man jailed for 10 years after he began sexually abusing a young girl when she was just six years old.

**_Convictions_**

**_Woman sentenced to life imprisonment following murder re-trial_**

A woman has been sentenced after being found guilty a second time of the murder of her partner who was killed in 2016.

Emma Magson was found guilty of the murder of James Knight in November 2016 and sentenced to life imprisonment ordering her to serve a minimum of 17 years.

Following an appeal against her conviction at the Court of Appeal in December 2019, a retrial was ordered which concluded at Birmingham Crown Court on Friday 5 March after eight weeks.

The jury in the second trial found Magson guilty of murder and she was sentenced on Monday 29 March to the same sentence - life imprisonment and to serve a minimum of 17 years.

_**Emma Magson**_

More information: https://www.leics.police.uk/news/leicestershire/news/2021/march/woman-sentenced-to-life-imprisonment-following-murder-re-trial/

**_Man sentenced to more than 12 years imprisonment following stabbing and for class A drugs offences_**

A man has been sentenced to more than 12 years imprisonment after being convicted of wounding with intent, possession of a knife and two counts of possession of class A drugs with intent to supply.

Nathan Bloor, 27, of Styon Road, Leicester, had been standing trial at Leicester Crown Court charged with wounding with intent and possession of a knife after a 25-year-man was stabbed in New Parks Way in January last year.

Prior to the trial, Bloor had pleaded guilty to possession with intent to supply heroin and possession with intent to supply crack cocaine. These charges relate to a separate incident on 2 June 2020

**_Ten-year sentence for man convicted of child sex offences_**

A man who began sexually abusing a young girl when she was just six years old has been jailed for 10 years.

Thirty-five-year-old Craig Townsend committed the offences in the Charnwood area across a number of years before 2018 when his victim contacted police and reported what had happened to her.

A full investigation was undertaken by detectives from the force's Child Abuse Investigation Unit.

_**Craig Townsend**_

**_Rapist jailed after city centre attack_**

A man who raped a women he befriended on a night out in the city centre was found guilty after a trial at Leicester Crown Court.

Following a media appeal 29-year-old Nathaniel Gray of Victoria Road East, Leicester, was identified, arrested and subsequently charged.

On Friday 5 March, following a five-day trial he was found guilty of rape.

He was sentenced to five years and six months in prison. He was also ordered to be placed on the Sex Offenders Register for life.

**_Man jailed after sexually assaulting woman and then sending hundreds of messages_**

A man who sexually assaulted a woman and then sent hundreds of messages to her has been sentenced to five years and two months imprisonment.

Wayne Lewis, 48, of Perth Avenue, Leicester, appeared at Leicester Crown Court for sentencing after previously pleading guilty to sexual assault and a charge of continuously engaging in behaviour which was controlling or coercive.

_**Wayne Lewis**_

Lewis also admitted breaching a Sexual Harm Prevention Order.

He was ordered to serve a total of five years and two months imprisonment. Following that, he will then serve four years and 10 months on extended licence.

He was also issued with a 10-year restraining order in relation to the victim and was made subject of a 10-year Sexual Harm Prevention Order.

**_Man jailed after supplying drugs in the Loughborough area_**

A man has been sent to prison for four years and one month after pleading guilty to supplying drugs in the Loughborough area.

Kennedy Ikhuenbor, 27, of North Street, Barrow-upon-Soar, was sentenced after officers discovered a phone line being used to sell heroin and crack cocaine.

It was found that between 1 June and 8 November 2020, the line was linked to the sale of class A drugs to as many as 75 individuals. Officers identified Ikhuenbor as the controller of the phone line.

Ikhuenbor had pleaded guilty to offences including possession with intent to supply class A drugs and was sentenced at Leicester Crown Court.

**_Men jailed after cannabis grow found by officers_**

Three men have been jailed after a cannabis grow was found in Leicester by officers executing a warrant.

Emirjan Qehaja, 28, Bexhet Tafhasi, 22, and Filiz Muja, 31, had all tried to flee the scene on foot during the early hours of Thursday 4 June last year after officers had executed a warrant under the Misuse of Drugs Act at a premises in Southgates.

But officers assisting the operation detained the men in the city centre a short while later.

A search of the premises was carried out and recovered a cannabis grow of around 600 plants.

All three pleaded guilty to conspiracy to produce class B drugs and were each sentenced to 20 months imprisonment.

More information: https://www.leics.police.uk/news/leicestershire/news/2021/march/men-jailed-after-cannabis-grow-found-by-officers/

**_Juvenile on electric scooter pleads guilty after injuring young boy_**

Officers are warning people about the dangers of electric scooters after a young boy was left with significant injuries.

Jamie Smith was walking with his father in Elston Fields, Leicester, last August, when the rider of an electric scooter collided with him.

Six-year-old Jamie was treated at the Queen's Medical Centre in Nottingham, for a fracture to his skull, and multiple cuts and bruises.

A 17-year-old, who can't be named for legal reasons, was charged with causing a serious injury by dangerous driving, failing to stop after a road traffic collision, failing to report a road traffic collision, using a motor vehicle without insurance, using a motor vehicle without a MOT certificate and driving a vehicle otherwise in accordance with a licence.

At Leicester Youth Court the defendant pleaded guilty to the charges. He will be sentenced on Monday 12 April.
