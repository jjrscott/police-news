Detectives investigating the death of a man in Wigston are continuing their enquiries.

Officers were called at 8.52pm on Thursday evening (11 February) to an address in Kingston Avenue following a report a man had sustained a stab wound injury.

Colleagues from East Midlands Ambulance Service attended the scene and the man, in his thirties, was pronounced dead a short time later.

A post mortem examination has been carried out and concluded the man died as a result of a stab wound.

A woman, in her 40s, who was arrested at the scene on suspicion of murder remains in custody where she is being questioned by detectives.

A cordon remains in place at the scene.

Detective Inspector Jenni Heggs from East Midlands Special Operations Unit (EMSOU) said: "Enquiries to determine the exact circumstances leading up to this incident are continuing.

"We can however confirm this is an isolated incident and we are not looking for anyone else in connection with it.

"Officers are carrying out house-to house enquiries in the neighbourhood and completing extensive CCTV trawls in the vicinity of the address to help us determine what happened while local officers are in the area offering reassurance to the community.

"If anyone has any information about this incident and has not yet spoken to us please get in touch.

"Anything you can tell us, regardless of how insignificant you think it might be, could assist with our investigation.

Anyone with information can call 101 quoting incident 551 of 11 February.
