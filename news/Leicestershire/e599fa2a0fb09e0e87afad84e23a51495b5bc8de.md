Officers have released CCTV footage to try and identify three men they wish to speak to in connection with a report of a stabbing on Granby Street, Leicester on Sunday 20 December.

Around 7pm, it was reported that a 17-year-old boy was approached by a man near Beaumont House on Granby Street and a fight broke out.

A second man was then reported to have joined the fight and the boy was stabbed a number of times on his body including his chest and leg.

It was reported a third man was with the group but not directly involved in the incident.

The victim was taken to Queen's Medical Centre, Nottingham, for treatment to his injuries. He has since been discharged from hospital.

The suspects were reported to have left the scene on foot towards Wharf Street South.

Detective Constable Robert Smith would like to speak to anyone who could help identify these men.

He said: "We are continuing with our enquiries to establish the full circumstances of this incident.

"This attack happened in the city centre when people may still have been out and about.

"We would urge anyone who may have witnessed the incident or who recognise the men pictured to make contact.

"Anyone with any other information which they believe could assist our enquiries should also get in touch."

Anyone with any information should contact 101 quoting incident 506 from Sunday 20 December.
