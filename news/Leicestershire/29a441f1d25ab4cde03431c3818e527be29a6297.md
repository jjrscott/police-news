Officers are appealing for information after a burglary in Wigston on Monday 15 March.

Around 10pm two men entered a commercial premises on Aylestone Lane and threatened staff with a knife and a hammer.

A quantity of cigarettes, tobacco and cash was stolen and carried away in a large holdall.

The men then reportedly left the scene on foot along Aylestone Lane, over the railway bridge and onto Goldhill Road.

One of the men is described as having a large build and wearing a white or grey hoody with the hood up, a black face covering, black gloves and shoes and green or khaki tracksuit bottoms.

The other man is described as wearing blue jeans, black trainers with a white trim around the bottom and gloves with a grey trim around the wrist.

Detective Constable Nick Robbins said: "We are carrying out investigations to try and identify these men.

"We would like to speak to anyone who may have seen them or anyone with dashcam footage that may have captured them."

Anyone with information should contact 101 quoting reference 21*146646.

You can also visit https://www.leics.police.uk/ro/report/ocr/af/how-to-report-a-crime/.
