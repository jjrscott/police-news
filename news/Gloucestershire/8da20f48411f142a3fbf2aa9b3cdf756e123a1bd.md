Thieves have stolen three charity tins from The Drillmans Arms pub in Cirencester over the weekend and police are appealing for information.

It was reported that unknown offenders smashed a window and forced entry to the pub where they stole three charity tins and two packets of cigarettes.

The charity tins stolen were for the Air Ambulance, Life Boats and Sooty charity for the blind.

Officers are appealing for witnesses to the burglary of the pub on Gloucester Road, Cirencester which took place early on Saturday 15 February, between 12:30am and 8:50am.

Anyone who was in the area at the time or witnessed this taking place is asked to call police on 101 quoting incident number 106 of 15 February.

Information can also be submitted to us online through our 'update a crime report' form on our website: https://www.gloucestershire.police.uk/tua/tell-us-about/cor/tell-us-about-existing-case-report/
