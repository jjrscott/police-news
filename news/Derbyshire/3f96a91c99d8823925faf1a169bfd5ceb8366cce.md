News

12:57 21/05/2020

Detectives investigating a robbery in Derby are appealing for the public's help in identifying three men.

At around 9.30pm on Friday 10 April three men forced their way into a flat in Crayford Road, Alvaston.

Inside they threatened and assaulted a man and a woman before stealing a number of pieces of jewellery.

Since the incident officers have been conducting a following number of lines of enquiry but are now asking for the assistance of the public in identifying the three men pictured.

The men are described as:

**SUSPECT 1** \- large distinctive SUPERDRY logo on the front of jumper.

****

**SUSPECT 2** \- Average build and wearing a light grey hooded jacket with the hood up, light blue or grey trousers and light-coloured trainers. He was wearing dark coloured gloves and was carrying a dark coloured ruck-sack.

**SUSPECT 3** \- Large/heavy build and wearing a dark navy-blue coat with a light coloured inner, blue coloured jeans and dark coloured footwear. He also appears to be wearing a dark coloured hat and dark coloured gloves.

Anyone with information about the incident, or who may be able to identify any of those pictured, is asked to contact Derbyshire police using any of the below methods, including reference 20*186828 in any correspondence:

 * Facebook- send us a private message to /DerbyshireConstabulary
 * Twitter- direct message our contact centre on @DerPolContact
 * Website- complete the online contact form derbyshire.police.uk/Contact-Us.
 * Phone - call us on 101.

You can also anonymously contact the independent charity Crimestoppers, on 0800 555 111 or by visiting www.crimestoppers-uk.org.
