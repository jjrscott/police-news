07:15 09/02/2021

A man has been jailed after he coughed, spat and assaulted hospital staff at the start of the first lockdown in Derby.

Man jailed after assaulting and coughing on hospital staff in Derby

Cory Harris had been admitted to the accident and emergency department at Royal Derby Hospital on Wednesday 8 April 2020 after he reported being assaulted. He had also been displaying symptoms of Coronavirus.

After the 35-year-old was told he was to be discharged and he needed to leave the hospital he became abusive. He removed his mask, threatened a nurse and began to run around the hospital.

Security staff were called to intervene, and Harris, of no fixed address, coughed, spat, bit and punched them, and officers were called.

Harris was arrested and later charged with affray and two counts of assaulting an emergency worker and remanded into custody.

During his trial, Harris also admitted to previous unrelated incidents of assault and possession of a knife in Derby and assaulting an emergency worker at HMP Lincoln.

He pleaded guilty and was sentenced to a total of three years imprisonment at a hearing at Derby Crown Court on Wednesday 3 February.
