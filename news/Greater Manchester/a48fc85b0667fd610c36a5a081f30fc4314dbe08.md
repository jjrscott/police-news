One man and one woman have been arrested following the discovery of a suspected methamphetamine production in Salford.

At around 8pm on Sunday 15 March 2020 police were called to reports of a disturbance on Bolton Road in Pendlebury.

Upon investigation officers discovered both equipment and chemicals believed to be used in the production of methamphetamine or 'Crystal Meth.'

A 43-year-old man and a 35-year-old woman have been arrested on suspicion of the production of class A drugs and remain in custody for questioning.

Inspector Daniel Welch of GMP's Salford district said: "Specialist officers continue to search the property and recover items used in the suspected production of methamphetamine at the scene.

"Although there is a higher police presence in the area I would like to stress there is no risk to members of the public. Our officers are continuing their investigations and ensuring the safety of the community."

Anyone who has any information should call police on 0161 856 5345 quoting incident number 2886 of 14/03/2020.

Details can also be passed on anonymously to the independent charity Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111.
