At around 12.29pm on Friday 25 September 2020, police were called to reports of a collision between a motorbike and a car on Pacific Way in Salford.

Enquiries have established that a Red Ducati Supersport S collided with a Grey Fiat 500.

The rider of the motorcycle - a 38-year-old man - was taken to hospital with serious injuries where he remains in a serious but stable condition.

The driver of the car - a 23-year-old woman - suffered minor injuries which were treated at the scene by paramedics.

No arrests have been made and enquiries remain ongoing.

Police Sergeant Matt Picton, of GMP's Serious Collision Investigation Unit, said: "This collision has sadly left a man with serious injuries and we wish him all the best for a speedy recovery.

"We are keen to establish the full circumstances surrounding this collision and would appeal to anyone who was in the area at the time and may have witnessed this incident, or anyone who may have any dash cam footage to please contact police.

"This collision happened in broad daylight on a busy stretch of road, so we are confident that there are a number of witnesses who may be able to assist with our enquiries."

Anyone with any information should contact police on 0161 856 4741 quoting incident number 1229 25/09/20.

Reports can also be made anonymously to the independent charity Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111.
