North Yorkshire Police is appealing for witnesses and information about a damages to plants that occurred in Thirsk Market Place.

It happened in between 3.19am and 3.22am on Sunday 2 August.

A white man in a light-coloured long-sleeved shirt was seen to get out of a slow moving vehicle and stagger up the market place. On his return, he uprooted plants from several pots. He then got back into the vehicle and the car drove off towards Millgate.

The vehicle was an '07 plate silver or light blue Ford Focus, with only one head light working and a large dent on the front bonnet.

We are conducting enquiries and are requesting the public's assistance to help determine the full circumstances surrounding the incident.

In particular, we are appealing for information about the vehicle.

Anyone with information that could assist the investigation should contact us on 101, select option 2, and ask for Nigel Warby. You can also email nigel.warby@northyorkshire.pnn.police.uk

If you wish to remain anonymous, you can pass information to Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111.

Please quote the North Yorkshire Police reference number NYP-02082015-0118
