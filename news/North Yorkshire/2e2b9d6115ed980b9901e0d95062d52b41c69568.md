Police are appealing for witnesses following a collision between a cement mixer wagon and a car at Catterick Bridge near Richmond.

The collision occurred near the Catterick Bridge Hotel at around 12pm on Tuesday 24 March 2015.

The wagon overturned on a bend and landed on the front of a Vauxhall Insignia.

The driver of the cement mixer, a 41-year-old man, was trapped in the cab and was freed by fire officers. He was airlifted to James Cook Hospital suffering from lacerations to his face. His injuries are not life-threatening.

The driver of the car, a man in his 50s, suffered minor injuries in the collision.

Anyone who witnessed the collision is urged to contact North Yorkshire Police on 101 - select option 2 - and ask for Traffic Constable Tony Leonard or Richmond Road Policing Group.

Alternatively, information can be emailed to tony.leonard@northyorkshire.pnn.police.uk

Please quote reference number NYP-24032015-0160 when passing information about this incident.
