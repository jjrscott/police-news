North Yorkshire Police are appealing for witnesses to a fail to stop collision which occurred at around midday on Wednesday 22 February 2017 in Harrogate.

A 34-year-old cyclist from the Harrogate area was riding his bike on Robert Street and had reached the junction with Station Parade, near Waitrose.

Whilst the cyclist was stationary at the junction waiting to turn right, a small, light coloured car traveled along Station Parade and turned right into Robert Street, but cut across the corner and drove into the waiting cyclist.

The driver, who was described as an elderly gentleman thought to be in his 70's, then drove away from the incident along Station Parade.

The cyclist was taken by ambulance to Harrogate Hospital where he was treated for leg injuries. He has since been discharged and recovering at home.

Anyone who was in the area at the time who witnessed the collision or who has any information which could help identify the driver is asked to contact TC 1656 Ken Riley on 101 or e-mail Ken.Riley@northyorkshire.pnn.police.uk quoting incident number 12170030638.
