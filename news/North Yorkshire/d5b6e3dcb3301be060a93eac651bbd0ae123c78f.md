Harrogate police are appealing for witnesses and information after a 14-year-old boy's was assaulted and had his bicycle stolen.

At approximately 8.15pm on Monday 31st October 2016, a 14-year-old boy was walking with his bike on Nydd Vale Terrace at the junction with Mayfield Terrace, Harrogate, when he was approached by a group of people . The group gathered around him before kicking and pulling at his bike and pulling it from his grip. The victim was then pushed and pulled about by the group before he managed to run away leaving his bike with the group.

We are conducting enquiries and are requesting the publicâ€™s assistance to help determine the full circumstances surrounding the incident.

In particular, we are appealing for information about any persons seen in the area at the time matching the descriptions of the persons involved before during or after the incident.

The suspects are described as being a group of approximately 6-7 people, all dressed in black clothing with hoods up, wearing plain white face masks with smiling faces on them.

Anyone with information that could assist the investigation should contact us on 101, select option 2, and ask for Pc 1519 Darren Copley . You can also email Darren.copley@northyorkshire.pnn.police.uk

If you wish to remain anonymous, you can pass information to Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111.

Please quote the North Yorkshire Police reference number 12160198713.
