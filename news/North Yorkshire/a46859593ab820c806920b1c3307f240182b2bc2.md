North Yorkshire Police is appealing for witnesses and information about an incident that occurred in York.

The incident happened outside KFC, Blossom Street, York, between 7.05pm and 7.15pm on Monday 18 March 2019, when a public order incident occurred involving a Deliveroo cyclist and a member of the public. The Deliveroo cyclist's bike was damaged in the incident.

We are requesting the public's assistance to help establish the full circumstances surrounding the incident. In particular, we are appealing for witnesses to come forward who saw the incident.

The cyclist was wearing a red bike helmet with black hood, dark clothing and had a green Deliveroo back pack on. The cyclist's bike was a race bike, red in colour and had curved white handlebars.

The member of the public was male and approximately 5ft 11ins tall, with a chubby build, wearing a burgundy beanie hat with an orange and white stripe. He was also wearing dark jeans, a black gilet and a grey long-sleeved top with sleeves rolled up, showing tattoos on his right arm.

Anyone with information that could assist the investigation should contact North Yorkshire Police on 101, select option 2, and ask for Benjamin Sykes. You can also email benjamin.sykes866@northyorkshire.pnn.police.uk

If you wish to remain anonymous, you can pass information to Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111.

Please quote the North Yorkshire Police reference number 12190049015.
