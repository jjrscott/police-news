Last modified: 11 November 2020 at 05:43pm

North Yorkshire Police has launched an investigation after a cannabis grow was uncovered in Richmond.

A police officer and a PCSO attended the property on Lyons Road at 4.48pm on Tuesday (10 November 2020) following a concern for safety report.

This initially resulted in a woman aged in her late 50s receiving treatment from the ambulance service for a minor hand injury.

However, the officers noticed a strong smell of cannabis in the property. Cannabis plants and associated cultivation equipment were located in another room.

A full search has been conducted at the property by the Operations Support Unit and all the suspicious items have been seized as part of the ongoing investigation.

A 26-year-old woman from Richmond was arrested at the scene on suspicion of being concerned in the supply of cannabis.

Following questioning in custody overnight, she was released under investigation while enquiries continue.

Anyone with information that could assist the investigation, including any recent suspicious behaviour on and around the Lyons Road area of Richmond, are urged to come forward as soon as possible.

Please call North Yorkshire Police on 101, select option 2, and ask for Michael Smith 0006. Or email Michael.Smith0006@northyorkshire.pnn.police.uk.

Alternatively, contact Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555111 or via www.crimestoppers-uk.org.

Please quote reference number 12200198155 when providing details about this investigation.
