Last modified: 27 December 2017 at 12:03pm

Police in York are appealing for witnesses following two burglaries and a collision in the early hours of Christmas Eve.

The two burglaries happened in Rosedale Avenue, York, and The Terrace in Rufforth.

Anyone who witnessed any suspicious activity in the areas between midnight and 4am on 24 December 2017, is asked to contact the police.

Officers are linking the burglaries with a collision in Rufforth at around 3.50am and would also like to hear from anyone who saw or heard a vehicle colliding with Rufforth village hall.

Two people – a man and a woman - were arrested following the collision. They have since been charged with two counts of burglary and aggravated vehicle taking. The man has also been charged with driving while disqualified and driving without insurance. They were due to appear at York Magistrates’ Court on 26 December.

Anyone who has any information about the burglaries or the collision and has not yet spoken to the police, is asked to contact North Yorkshire Police on 101, select option 2 and ask for PC Nicola Gill or email Nicola.gill@northyorkshire.pnn.police.uk

If you wish to remain anonymous, you can pass information to Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111.

Please quote reference number 12170228970 when passing on information.
