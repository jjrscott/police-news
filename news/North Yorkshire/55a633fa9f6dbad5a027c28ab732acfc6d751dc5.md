Last modified: 28 September 2017 at 06:21pm

Police are appealing for witnesses and dash-cam footage after a 12-year-old girl was seriously injured when she was hit by a car in Byram near Knottingley.

The collision happened at around 3.30pm on Thursday 28 September 2017, when the girl got off her school bus on West Acres, crossed the road and was hit by a red Vauxhall Astra that failed to stop at the scene.

Officers are appealing to anyone who was in Byram this afternoon and either witnessed the incident or saw a red Vauxhall Astra travelling through the village, to contact them.

In addition they would like anyone with dash-cam footage of the collision or of the Vauxhall Astra to contact them.

Anyone with information should contact North Yorkshire Police on 101.

If you do not want to give your name, you can contact Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111.

Please quote reference 313 of 28 September when passing on information.

The victim has been airlifted to Leeds General Infirmary where she currently remains.

Police enquiries led officers to arrest a local man in his thirties and recover the suspect vehicle in Knottingley.

The arrested man remains in police custody at this time.

The road is currently closed while officers carry out an examination of the scene.
