UPDATED 8.30am 24 September 2020

Following the incident in York on 23 September when a car entered the River Ouse in York, the vehicle was recovered from the water at around 1am on 24 September.

The driver of the car, a woman in her 40s was taken to hospital but was not seriously injured.

The car had no other occupants inside.

An underwater search of the area was carried out to check for any other people, and officers are satisfied at this time that there were no other occupants.

The scene has now been cleared.

Enquiries are ongoing into the circumstances that led to the car entering the water.

**23 September 2020**

Emergency services are at the scene of an incident at Skeldergate Bridge in York after a car entered the River Ouse.

It was reported at 4.30pm on 23 September 2020.

One person from the car is safe, however, it is still unconfirmed how many people were in the vehicle when it entered the water.

The Yorkshire and Humber Marine and Underwater Search Unit are currently at the scene.

The road is currently closed to allow emergency services to deal with the incident.

Incident number: NYP-23092020-00346
