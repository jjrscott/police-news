Last modified: 25 June 2018 at 12:57pm

Officers from the Roads Policing Group are appealing for witnesses to a serious collision, which has left a rider with life changing injuries, to contact them.

Police were contacted at 2.55pm on Sunday 24 June 2018 to reports of a collision involving a red Vauxhall Vectra and a red Ducati Panigale V4 motorbike, between Escrick and Wheldrake on Wheldrake Lane near York.

The motorbike rider, a 67-year-old man from Pontefract, sustained serious injuries to his legs and was taken to Leeds General Infirmary by air ambulance, where he remains in a serious condition.

The driver of the Vauxhall, a 77-year-old man from Escrick, was unhurt.

The road was closed to allow the Collision Investigation Team to examine the scene and for the vehicles to be recovered. The road was reopened at 7.15pm.

Officers are conducting enquiries to establish the circumstances around the incident and are requesting anyone who witnessed the collision, or saw either the car or the motorbike prior to the incident to contact them.

Please dial 101, select option 2 and ask to speak to Traffic Constable Jamie Lord or email Jamie.Lord@northyorkshire.pnn.police.uk

Please quote reference 12180112955.
