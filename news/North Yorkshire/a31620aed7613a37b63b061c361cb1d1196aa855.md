Last modified: 5 March 2019 at 05:15pm

Police are continuing to investigate a suspected arson incident in Barlby at the weekend.

It occurred at a property on Acorn Close at around 10.20pm on Saturday (2 March 2019) and resulted in the destruction of a caravan and a shed.

Two large gas explosions spread debris over a 125 metre radius.

A 31-year old man local man was arrested on suspicion of arson and harassment. Following questioning he was released on conditional police bail, which includes staying away from the area, while enquiries continue.

Anyone with information about the incident, particularly any suspicious behaviour in the Acorn Close area from around 10pm on Saturday 2 March, is urged to come forward as soon as possible.

Also, officers want to speak to anyone who believes their property has been damaged by the debris form the explosions.

Please contact North Yorkshire Police on 101, select option 1, and speak to the Force Control Room.

If you would prefer to remain anonymous, contact Crimestoppers on 0800 555111.

Please quote reference number 12190038804 when providing details about this incident.
