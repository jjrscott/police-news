North Yorkshire Police is appealing for witnesses and information about a dog attack in Scarborough.

It happened on Gladstone Lane, Scarborough at approximately 7.50 pm on Saturday 5th May 2018 and involved a dog, that was off the lead, approach a member of the public, jump and bite the victim on the torso before chasing the victim down the street.

The dog is described as a large Boxer dog and was with a male aged in his late 20's to early 30's.

We are requesting the publicâ€™s assistance to help establish the full circumstances surrounding the incident. If this was you and your dog, I urge you to get in contact to work together to prevent any further incidents of a similar nature. I am equally keen to hear from any person who may have information that could assist the investigation.

Anyone with information that could assist the investigation should contact North Yorkshire Police on 101, select option 2, and ask for PC 1092 Nick Simpson. You can also email nick.simpson@northyorkshire.pnn.police.uk

If you wish to remain anonymous, you can pass information to Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111.
