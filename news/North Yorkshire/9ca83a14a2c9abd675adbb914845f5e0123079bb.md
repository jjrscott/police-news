North Yorkshire Police is appealing for witnesses and information about an assault that occurred on Lendal Bridge in York city centre.

The incident happened on Lendal Bridge in York near to the junction with Rougier Street at 5.20pm on Tuesday 13 June 2017 and involved a verbal altercation and physical assault between a male cyclist and a male pedestrian.

The male pedestrian sustained facial injuries in the assault, which were treated at York District Hospital. He was later discharged following treatment.

We are conducting enquiries and are requesting the publicâ€™s assistance to help determine the full circumstances surrounding the incident.

In particular, we are appealing for information about the identity of the male cyclist who is described as a white, about 6 ft 1 tall, of slim build with short dark hair and short dark facial hair. He was wearing a black T-shirt and black trousers, and riding a dark framed road bike at the time of the incident.

Anyone with information that could assist the investigation should contact us on 101, select option 2, and ask for Ffion Twomlow. You can also email ffion.twomlow001941@northyorkshire.pnn.police.uk

If you wish to remain anonymous, you can pass information to Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111.

Please quote the North Yorkshire Police reference number 12170103306.
