A man wearing a white top has been identified following a CCTV appeal in connection with the theft of a motorhome from Barlow near Selby.

Officers have thanked members of the public for their assistance with the appeal.

The image of the man wearing a white top has now been removed to allow the justice process to take its course.

A second man shown in an earlier appeal wearing an orange hi-vis jacket has come forward and has been eliminated from the investigation.

12.53pm 27 Nov 2018

Previous appeal Issued 9.38am 27 Nov 2018

Man in CCTV appeal eliminated from stolen motorhome investigation - police re-appeal to find man in white top

Police investigating the theft of a motorhome from Barlow near Selby have eliminated a member of the public following a CCTV appeal as they continue their appeal for information.

Officers issued an appeal to identify two men, including a man in an orange hi-vis jacket. The man in the orange jacket has come forward and has been eliminated from police enquiries, it has also been established that he did not arrive at the location with the other man in the CCTV images.

Officers are still appealing to anyone who can help identify the man shown in the white top.

The white, Ford Katamarano motorhome was stolen from the driveway of a property in Barlow at around 2pm on Monday 19 November 2018.

Unfortunately the vehicle was found burnt out on Thursday 22 November 2018, by officers in the Nottinghamshire area.

Anyone who has any information that could help identify the man in the CCTV image is asked to contact North Yorkshire Police on 101 and ask to speak to PC 1810 Dan Kirven. Please quote reference 12180216339.

Crimestoppers can be contacted anonymously on 0800 555 111.
