Police were called just after 6.30am on Monday 15 February 2016 to reports that a cleaner at a local business premises had seen a man fall from Ouse Bridge in York into the river.

All emergency services attended the area, however, there was no sign of anyone in the water or any further witnesses to the incident.
With the help of the person who reported the incident, officers retraced steps back to the exact location of the jump. They then viewed an hours worth of high quality CCTV footage which covered the area and the time of the reported incident. There was nothing on the

CCTV showing a person jumping off the bridge.
Officers then carried out door to door enquiries with businesses in the area but no other witnesses have been found.
If anyone has any further information or saw anything untoward on Ouse Bridge between 5.30am and 6.35am on Monday 15 February 2016, we urge you get in touch.
There have been no reports of missing people during this time and anyone who is missing someone is also urged to contact the police.

Please call 101 and pass information to the Force Control Room quoting incident number 12160027246
