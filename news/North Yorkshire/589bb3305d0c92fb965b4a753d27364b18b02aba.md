Last modified: 24 January 2017 at 08:47am

Police are appealing for information and witnesses following an attempted robbery at the Subway shop on Gowthorpe in Selby.

It happened at around 8.30pm on the night of Sunday 22 January 2017 when a man entered the shop and threatened a member of staff with a knife and demanded money from the till.

The member of staff refused to hand over any money and the suspect left the shop empty-handed.

The suspect is described as white, aged in his mid-twenties to mid-thirties, over 5ft, 7in tall and of slim build. He was wearing a black scarf covering his face, a black beanie hat with a black hooded top over the hat and long sleeves. He wore black gloves, black jeans and white trainers.

Anyone who was in the area between 8.25pm and 8.55pm and witnessed the incident or saw a man matching the suspect's description is asked to contact North Yorkshire Police on 101, select option 2 and ask for York Serious Crime Team.

If you wish to remain anonymous, you can pass information to Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111.

Please quote reference 12170012535 when passing on information.
