Here PCSO Tess Forsdyke based in Malton answers a few Q&As; about being a PCSO.

Q: How long have you been a PCSO?

A: 3 years

Q: Where are you based?

A: Malton

Q: What did you do before becoming a PCSO?

A: I was a carer and I worked with horses.

Q: What do you do in your spare time?

A: First and foremost I am a single Mum of 2 children, I spend my time with them and enjoy trips out with them. Further to that I love walking with my dogs, shooting, cycling (I am doing a charity ride this year) and exploring new places.

Q: What made you decide to apply to become a PCSO?

A: Always wanted to be in the police.

Q: What was your first day like?

A: Terrifying but exciting

Q: Favourite thing about being a PCSO?

A: The variety and chance to help people

Q: Worst thing about being a PCSO?

A: Sometimes the negativity that we get from people.

Q: Three words that sum up what being a PCSO is really like?

A: Rewarding, different, amazing

Q: One thing that people wouldnâ€™t know about PCSOs?

A: We are not volunteers.

Q: Advice for anyone who is thinking of become a PCSO?

A: Apply because you want to do the job, not just because you want to be a police officer.

For more details or to apply to become a PCSO visit northyorkshire.police.uk/pcso (closing date Friday 21 April, 9am)

If you have any questions for Tess or would like to speak to her about becoming a PCSO feel free to email her on Tess.Forsdyke@northyorkshire.pnn.police.uk

Good luck!
