Scarborough police are appealing for information following a knife-point robbery.

It happened on Friars Way, Scarborough at around 8.15pm on Friday 19 May 2017 opposite the council car park.

The victim, an 18-tear-old man, who was working as a delivery driver for a local takeaway and had just made a delivery on Friars Way and was returning to his car, was approached by a man with a knife who demanded that the victim give him money.

The victim handed over his wallet which is described as a black leather flip wallet with green stitching and the letter “W” on it.

The suspect is described as a white man and he was dressed in all black with his hood up. The lower part of his face was covered with tightly fitting black material.

Police are appealing to anyone who witnessed the incident or has any information about the suspect to contact them.

Anyone with any information that can help the police investigation, 0r can help to locate the wallet, is asked to contact North Yorkshire Police on 101, select option 2 and ask for Graeme Boast. You can also email Graeme.Boast@northyorkshire.pnn.police.uk

If you wish to remain anonymous, you can pass information to Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111.

Please quote reference 12170086373 when calling.
