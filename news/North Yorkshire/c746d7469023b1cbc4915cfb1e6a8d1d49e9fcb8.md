Last modified: 6 January 2020 at 05:20pm

North Yorkshire Police is appealing for information following a collision between a pedestrian and a car.

Around 6pm on Saturday 4 January 2020, a black BMW X1 collided with a pedestrian. The incident occurred on the A170 Stepney Road in Scarborough, just after the junction with Stepney Avenue, as the vehicle was travelling away from the town.

The driver of the BMW, a 39 year old man from Scarborough was uninjured. Sadly, the pedestrian, a 62 year old man also from Scarborough was pronounced deceased at the scene. The pedestrian was accompanied by a 31 year old man who sustained minor injuries.

The road was closed for just over six hours whilst police collision investigators examined the scene and was reopened shortly after 12.30am.

Police are appealing for anyone who may have witnessed the collision or seen the two men walking up Stepney Road from the direction of the Crown Tavern towards the 6th form college beforehand. We would also like to speak to anyone who assisted at the scene or anyone with dashcam footage who may have been in the area in the time leading up to the collision.

Witnesses are asked to contact the Major Collision Investigation Team on 101 or by email; MCIT@northyorkshire.pnn.police.uk quoting the incident reference 12200002218.
