Police in York are appealing for witnesses and information about a burglary at a farm in Huntington.

It happened just off of North Lane, towards the east-bound A64, between Friday 25 September and Sunday 27 September 2020.

The victim had various high-value goods stolen from secured shipping containers.

Several heavy-duty padlocked gates had been broken into to access the farm.

The suspects also broke into a caravan on a field and ransacked it looking for items to steal.

Having carried out a full inventory of the victim's belongings, officer identified the following items had been taken:

 * A white and red 249cc Honda motor bike with a distinct tiger badge on the fuel tank, reg-number L989ENB
 * A tool bag containing a mixture of sheep shearing equipment and miscellaneous tools
 * A red Mig gas-less welder, measuring 61cm x 20cm
 * a red two stroke mini generator, recognisable by the charging cord handle which had been replaced by a black peg

Anyone with information that could assist the investigation should contact North Yorkshire Police on 101, select option 2, and ask for Dominic Drumm. Or email dominic.drumm@northyorkshire.pnn.police.uk.

If you wish to remain anonymous, you can pass information to Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111.

Please quote the North Yorkshire Police reference number 12200170141 when providing details.
