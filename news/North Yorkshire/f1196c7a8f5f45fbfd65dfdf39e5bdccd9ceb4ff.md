Officers are appealing for information after criminal damage was caused to a residential property on Alcuin Avenue, Tang Hall, York.

The offence occurred just before 10.50pm on Wednesday, 17 June when two teenage boys have approached the property from the direction of Melrose Gate. One the boys has then picked up a piece of gravel from the driveway and thrown it at a downstairs window resulting in around Â£60 worth of damage.

Both boys left in the direction of the Etty Avenue/Tang Hall Lane area.

No injuries were reported.

The first boy was described as a white teenager, wearing a white t-shirt, a dark hooded top (which was open at the time of the offence), jeans and white trainers. This person threw the stone at the window of the property.

The second boy was described as white, wearing an all grey tracksuit (with the hood up) and black trainers. He was taller than the first.

Officers in the area conducted a search for the offenders but they were not located.

If you have your suspicions around someone you know or believe you may know the identity of the offenders please contact North Yorkshire Police. The incident was captured on CCTV which may be released at a future date.

Anyone with information that could assist the investigation should contact North Yorkshire Police on 101. You can also email dominic.drumm@northyorkshire.pnn.police.uk

If you wish to remain anonymous, you can pass information to Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111.

Please quote the North Yorkshire Police reference number 12200102626.
