North Yorkshire Police is appealing for witnesses and information about an assault that occurred on Church Street in York.

It happened on Church Street at 9.45pm on Wednesday 25 January.

The victim was walking to meet his wife when he became involved in a verbal argument with three people sat on a bench outside Camera Mart on Church Street. Two males then assaulted the victim, who was punched and kicked while he was on the floor, causing injuries including a swollen eye which the victim could not open for several days, bruised back and arms and several facial cuts. The victim attended York Hospital and received treatment for these injuries.

We are conducting enquiries and are requesting the publicâ€™s assistance to help determine the full circumstances surrounding the incident.

In particular, we are appealing for information from anyone who was in that area and saw the assault, or any of the suspects.

Suspect 1 described as a white male, approximately 40-45 years old, about 5ft 10ins and a stocky build. He was described as having short, dark greying hair with facial stubble, wearing a dark jacket and blue jeans.

Suspect 2 is described as a white male, approximately 40-45 years old and 6ft tall and a slender build. He had shaved blond/ginger hair.

The third person is described as a white female, approximately 40 years old, around 5ft 5ins tall and average build.

Anyone with information that could assist the investigation should contact us on 101, select option 2, and ask for Harriet Shillito. You can also email harriet.shillito@northyorkshire.pnn.police.uk

If you wish to remain anonymous, you can pass information to Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111.

Please quote the North Yorkshire Police reference number 12170014548.
