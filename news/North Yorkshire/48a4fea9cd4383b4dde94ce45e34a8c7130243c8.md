Police are appealing for witnesses following a collision in Selby involving a car and a pedal cycle.

The collision occurred at about 6.10am on 26 June when a black Vauxhall Insignia collided with a cyclist after an altercation on Barlby Road.

The driver of the Insignia left the scene without stopping to leave their details.

The cyclist, a 52 year-old local man, manged to get himself to hospital where he received treatment for three broken ribs and a partially collapsed lung.

Police are particularly keen to speak to anyone who witnessed the collision or who was in the area at the time that may have dash-cam footage.

Anyone with information is urged to contact North Yorkshire Police on 101, select option 2 and ask for Richard Stagg, or email Richard.Stagg@northyorkshire.pnn.police.uk. Please quote reference number 12180118923.
