Appeal for information following a collision with a pedestrian on Lendal in York

The collision happened between 1.15-1.30pm on Wednesday 27 July 2016 outside The Post Office on Lendal, York. A cyclist was seen to be cycling the wrong way down Lendal, through a large number of pedestrians when a collision occured with a male pedestrian.

The cyclist was then seen to get off his bike and approach the pedestrian, and push him in the chest, causing him to fall over. The fall resulted in the pedestrian receiving a fractured leg. The cyclist was then seen to get back on his bike and cycle away in the direction of Coney Street.

The cyclist is described as male, of slim build and 5'6" in height, with dark, chin length hair which was described as slightly wavy. He was described as having an unkempt, weathered appearance with discoloured teeth and spoke with a Yorkshire accent. He was wearing dark coloured clothes which consisted of a short sleeved top worn under a sleeveless, gilet type jacket.

Anyone who witnessed the incident, or recognises the description of the man is asked to contact 101, select option 2 and ask to speak to DC Lindsay Bell. Please quote reference 12160134102. You can also email Lindsay.Bell@northyorkshire.pnn.police.uk

Alternatively you can contact Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111.
