Update: The man pictured on CCTV has now been identified and is helping with our enquiries. Thank you to all who shared our appeal.

**Original release: CCTV appeal following vehicle damage in Copmanthorpe**

North Yorkshire Police has issued CCTV images of a man and two women they would like to speak to after a number of vehicles were damaged in Copmanthorpe.

It happened at approx. 3.30am on Sunday 19 March 2017 on Fairfax Way, Barons Crescent and St Giles Way and involved a number of vehicles being scratched by a sharp implement.

We are conducting enquiries and are requesting the publicâ€™s assistance to help determine the full circumstances surrounding the incident.

In particular, we would like to speak to the people in the CCTV stills as they may have important information that could assist the investigation.

The man is described as being in his late 20s, early 30s, about 6 foot tall with dark, slightly receding hair. He was wearing a dark jacket, light coloured knee lenght shorts and black Nike trainers.

Anyone who recognises the people in the CCTV images, or who has any information that could assist the investigation should contact us on 101, select option 2 and ask to speak to Terence Priestman.

If you wish to remain anonymous, you can pass information to Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111.

Please quote the North Yorkshire Police reference number **12170046290**
