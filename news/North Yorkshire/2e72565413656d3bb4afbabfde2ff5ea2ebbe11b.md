Last modified: 17 October 2018 at 04:33pm

North Yorkshire Police are investigating a recent incident involving the iconic statue of Constantine which stands between York Minster and the Church of St Michael le Belfry.

Around 11pm on Wednesday 28 September 2016, officers were called to a report of a man brandishing a sword which had been taken from the statue of Constantine.

The statue was commissioned by the York Civic Trust in 1998 and stands between the Minster and the Church of St Michael le Belfry.

A man was detained nearby and arrested on suspicion of causing damage to the statue. He remains in police custody.

The missing sword was recovered by a member of the public and was passed to police officers.

Anyone who may have witnessed the incident is urged to contact PC1597 Pete McFarlane of the York Investigation Hub on 101, quoting incident number 12160177029.

If you wish to remain anonymous, you can pass information to Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111.
