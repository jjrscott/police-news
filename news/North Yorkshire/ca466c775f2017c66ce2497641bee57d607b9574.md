Last modified: 7 October 2016 at 05:26pm

Police in Scarborough are appealing for information after a 13-year-old girl was hit by a car in Snainton near Scarborough on the afternoon of Friday 7 October 2016.

It happened at around 3.30pm on the High Street when the girl got of the school bus.

As she crossed the road she was hit by a car that failed to stop at the scene.

Thankfully her injuries were not serious and she did not require hospital treatment at the time.

Police are appealing to the driver of the car to come forward and to anyone who was in the area of Snainton High Street at around 3.30pm to contact them.

Anyone with any information is asked to contact North Yorkshire Police on 101, select option 2 and ask fro Traffic Constable Mark Hutchings, or email mark.hutchings@northyorkshire.pnn.police.uk

If you wish to remain anonymous, you can pass information to Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111.

Please quote incident number 358 of 7 October.
