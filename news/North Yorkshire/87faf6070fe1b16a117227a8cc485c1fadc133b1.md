Last modified: 17 October 2018 at 04:34pm

North Yorkshire Police is appealing for witnesses and information about a road traffic collision that occurred on The Stonebow in York.

The collision happened at 11.10pm on Sunday 3 July 2016 and involved a small black-coloured car colliding with a pedestrian in the road.

The pedestrian sustained serious injuries to his legs and was taken to York District Hospital by ambulance. The pedestrian has since been discharged from hospital, having received treatment.

A vehicle matching the description of the small black-coloured car was stopped in the city and the driver has been spoken to about the collision, however police are appealing to anyone who may have witnessed the collision or a small black-coloured car driving in the area, to get in touch to assist the investigation.

If you can help, please contact us on 101, select option 2, and ask for Christopher Storey. You can also email Christopher.Storey@northyorkshire.pnn.police.uk

Please quote the North Yorkshire Police reference number 12160117503.
