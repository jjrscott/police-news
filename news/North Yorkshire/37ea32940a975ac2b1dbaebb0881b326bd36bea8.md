Police have issued details of a large amount of horse tack stolen from stables in Seamer.

Items worth a total of about Â£800 were taken from Pasture Lane, Seamer, some time between 6.30pm on Wednesday 16 September and 7am on Thursday 17 September 2015.

Among the stolen tack was a GFS Fieldhouse general purpose saddle, 17 inches wide. This saddle has a North Yorkshire Police security stamp, number R0215.

Also taken was a brown general purpose saddle, as well as reins, stirrups, bridles and other tack.

Anyone with information about the burglary, or who is offered the stolen items for sale, is asked to contact North Yorkshire Police by dialling 101, selecting option 2 and asking for Scarborough Police.

Alternatively, you can contact the independent charity Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555111 or through their Anonymous Online Form at www.crimestoppers-uk.org. No personal details are taken, information cannot be traced or recorded and you will not go to court.

Please quote reference number 12150164322 when passing information about this incident.

Horse owners and riders are being urged to visit the Horsewatch page of North Yorkshire Police's online Rural Crime Hub at www.northyorkshire.police.uk/horsewatch for crime prevention advice, including details of security marking services available for tack.
