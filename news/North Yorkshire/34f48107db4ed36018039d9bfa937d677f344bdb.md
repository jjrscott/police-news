North Yorkshire Police is appealing for witnesses and information about a dog attack that occurred in Brayton Barff, Brayton, Selby.

It happened at Brayton Barff at approximately 4.45pm on Wednesday 20 June 2018 and involved a tan coloured large, solid-build Bull Mastiff/Staffordshire terrier type dog.

The dog was not on a lead. The dog attacked a Jack Russell which was on a lead with its owner. The dog also bit the hand of the Jack Russell owner.

At a result of the incident, the injured dog was taken to a vets and received staples to its neck. Its owner attended Selby Hospital and was treated for lacerations to his hand.

If you wish to remain anonymous, you can pass information to Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111.
