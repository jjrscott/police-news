Last modified: 20 October 2020 at 05:03pm

Police are appealing for information and dash-cam footage after a pedestrian died in a collision on the A166 near Stamford Bridge.

It was reported at 7.14pm on Friday 16 October when a 45-year-old man was involved in a collision with a grey Fiat 500.

Sadly the man sustained fatal injuries in the collision and died at the scene.

Officers are appealing to anyone who saw a man walking along the A166 between Stamford Bridge and Gate Helmsley, or saw a small grey car travelling along the A166 either prior to the collision or anyone who witnessed the collision, to come forward. Officers are also appealing to anyone with dash-cam footage of the man, the grey car or the collision.

Anyone who can assist the investigation is asked to contact North Yorkshire Police on 101, select option 2 and ask for the Major Collision Investigation Team or email mcit@northyorkshire.pnn.police.uk

Please quote reference 12200181961 when passing on information.

The road was closed are reopened at around 12.30am.

The driver of the car was physically uninjured.
