Police are appealing for information after an antique pistol was stolen from a National Trust property near York.

The declassified English percussion pocket pistol, which is one of a pair, was taken from Nunnington Hall, Nunnington, at sometime between 1 June 2015 and 16 June 2015.

The pistol, which dates back to 1840, measures 7.8cm and was stolen from the wall in the property's Stone Hall.

Officers have released images of the stolen pistol and are appealing to anyone with information about the theft to come forward.

Anyone who can help officers with their enquiries is urged to contact North Yorkshire Police on 101 - select option 2 - and ask for PC Paul Fricker.

Alternatively, information can be emailed topaul.fricker@northyorkshire.pnn.police.uk

If you prefer not to give your name, Crimestoppers can be contacted anonymously on 0800 555 111.

Please quote reference number 12150100654 when passing information about this incident.
