Last modified: 5 August 2018 at 12:04pm

Three men have been arrested on suspicion of attempted murder after a stabbing outside the Square Bar in Selby.

Police were contacted at 2.36am Sunday 5 August 2018 to reports of a stabbing where a 21-year-old man from Selby had received injuries to his chest and a 20-year-old man from Selby had received injuries to his wrist. Both men were taken to Leeds General Infirmary for treatment for their injuries, which are not thought to be life threatening. The 20-year-old man has since been released from hospital.

Two further males were taken to York District Hospital with injuries, one of which requires surgery.

A 26-year old man was arrested locally arrested on suspicion of attempted murder and possession of a Class A drug. He currently remains in police custody.

A vehicle believed to have been involved with the incident, was located at 3.40am by police, travelling along the A63 and continuing onto the A1. Following a short pursuit, where the vehicle failed to stop, it was involved in a collision with a unrelated vehicle in the Glasshoughton area of West Yorkshire. The driver of the unrelated vehicle received minor injuries.

Two men in the vehicle were arrested on suspicion of attempted murder and currently remain in the custody of West Yorkshire Police.
