Last modified: 16 October 2018 at 11:18am

Police in Thirsk are appealing for witnesses and information after a man was punched and fell to ground in Thirsk Market Place.

The assault happened between 1.45am and 1.50am in the early hours of Sunday 11 June 2017, and involved two men outside the Arabica café in the market place.

The victim, a man aged in his twenties, was punched once to his face causing him to fall to the ground, resulting in him sustaining a serious injury to his jaw.

Officers are conducting enquiries and are requesting the public’s assistance to help determine the full circumstances surrounding the incident.

In particular, officers are appealing for information about the identity of those involved.

Anyone who was out in Thirsk on Saturday night into Sunday morning and was either in the market place or any of the local pubs near Arabica, witnessed the incident, or has any information about those involved, is asked to contact the police.

Anyone with information that could assist the investigation should contact us on 101, select option 2, and ask for PC1817 Mark Atkinson You can also email mark.atkinson@northyorkshire.pnn.police.uk

If you wish to remain anonymous, you can pass information to Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111.

Please quote the North Yorkshire Police reference number 12170101768.
