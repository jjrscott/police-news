Stokesley Police are appealing for witnesses and information about a several offences of criminal damage to the same vehicle that have occurred over recent months in Great Ayton.

The most recent damage occured overnight 24-25th January 2016 when the vehicle was parked at the roadside on Romany Road in Great Ayton and involved the offender deliberately scratching the paintwork of the black Hyundai car with an unknown implement along the drivers door causing approximately Â£100 worth of damage

Sgt Nick Hill of Stokesley Safer Neighbourhood Team said "This is the sixth time in the last four months the victim's car has been damaged, each time in a similar manner and overnight. To date we have not been able to establish any motive for this damage being caused but are committed to tracking down the person/s responsible. Those responsible have cost the victim several hundred pounds in repairs"

"We are stepping up patrols in the area and are conducting enquiries into these offences and are requesting the public's assistance to help determine the identity of the offender/s."

"In particular, we are appealing for information from anyone whom has seen anyone acting suspiciously around vehicles in the Street or from anyone whom could provide us with a motive for these offences"

Anyone with information that could assist the investigation should contact us on 101, select option 2, and ask for PC 1844 Ballantyne. You can also email charlotte.ballantyne@northyorkshire.pnn.police.uk

If you wish to remain anonymous, you can pass information to Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111.

Please quote the North Yorkshire Police reference number 12160014015
