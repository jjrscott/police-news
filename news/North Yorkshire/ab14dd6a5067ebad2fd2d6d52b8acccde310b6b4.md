Last modified: 27 October 2017 at 04:36pm

Police are now in a position to name the man who died as a result of a collision on the B1257 near Stokesley on Wednesday 25 October 2017. He was 19-year-old Jordan Bowes of Great Broughton.

Jordan was driving a blue Audi Quattro at 10.20am on the B1257, when a collision occurred with a white Mercedes box van. Jordan very sadly died at the scene.

Police are appealing for any witnesses who were travelling along the B1257 at the time of the incident, who may have captured dash-cam footage or witnessed the two vehicles, to get in touch. Please contact North Yorkshire Police on 101, select option 2 and ask to speak to Hamish Halloway.

Alternatively you can email hamish.halloway@northyorkshire.pnn.police.uk

Please quote reference number 12170191381.

Original Appeal released Wednesday 25 October 2017 at 2:49pm:

**Appeal for witnesses to fatal collision on B1257 near Stokesley** **
**
Police were contacted at 10.20am Wednesday 25 October 2017 by Yorkshire Ambulance Service regarding a fatal collision on the B1257 near Stokesley, between a white Mercedes box van and a blue Audi Quattro. Sadly a 19-year-old man died at the scene.

Police are currently in attendance at the scene and the road has been closed to allow a full investigation to take place.

Police are appealing for any witnesses who were travelling along the B1257 at the time of the incident who may have seen the two vehicles, captured dash-cam footage or witnessed the collision to contact North Yorkshire Police on 101, select option 2 and ask to speak to Hamish Halloway.

Alternatively you can email hamish.halloway@northyorkshire.pnn.police.uk

Please quote reference number 12170191381.
