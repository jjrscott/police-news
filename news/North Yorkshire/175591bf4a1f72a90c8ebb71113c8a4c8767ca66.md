Last modified: 11 October 2016 at 09:12am

Police in York are appealing for information following an assault near York Racecourse on the evening of Friday 7 October 2016.

It happened at 6.40pm on Racecourse Road when the victim, who has not yet come forward, was walking with another man and was assaulted by the driver of a car who drove past them and got out of his vehicle before hitting the man.

The victim, who is believed to be aged in his 60s or 70s, was left on the ground with head injuries.

Police appealing to anyone who was in the area at the time and saw what happened to contact them. They are also appealing for the victim to come forward.

A concerned member of the public reported the incident to the police and a 21-year-old man from Leeds was arrested in connection with the incident. He has been released on police bail while enquiries continue.

If you are the victim, or if you witnessed the incident, please call North Yorkshire Police on 101, select option 2 and ask for PC Neel Seth or email neel.seth@northyorkshire.pnn.police.uk

If you wish to remain anonymous, you can pass information to Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111.

Please quote reference number 12160182814 when passing on information.
