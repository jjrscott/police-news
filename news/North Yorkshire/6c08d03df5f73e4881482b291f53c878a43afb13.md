North Yorkshire Police is trying to trace a man pictured by CCTV after a house in Malton was broken in to.

Sentimentally-valuable pieces of jewellery were taken from the house, on East Mount, along with a quantity of cash.

A man in his 20s has been arrested in connection with the incident and released under investigation while enquiries continue.

However, police would like to speak to a second man who was pictured by CCTV.

He is described as white, in his late teens or early twenties and of proportionate build.

He was wearing a grey hooded top, blue jeans and multi-coloured trainers. He is about 5ft 8ins tall and has short dark hair.

The incident took place on the afternoon of July 21 2017.

Anyone who recognises the man pictured by CCTV or has any other information should contact North Yorkshire Police on 101, select option 2, and ask for Steve Williams. You can also email Detetive Constable Williams at steve.williams@northyorkshire.pnn.police.uk

If you wish to remain anonymous, you can pass information to Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111.

Please quote reference number 12170128456 when passing on information.
