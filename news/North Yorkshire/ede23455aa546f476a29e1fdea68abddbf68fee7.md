York police are appealing for information after a man was struck in the face with a glass inside Kennedy's Caf√© Bar on Little Stonegate.

At around 9.10pm on Sunday 5 April 2015, the victim, a man aged in his 20s from Goole, was stood at the bar with friends when he was struck in the face with a glass.

The glass shattered on impact, causing lacerations to his face and eyelid. He was taken to York District Hospital for treatment to his injuries including stitches to his eyelid which will require follow-up visits to ensure no permanent damage has been caused.

The suspect is believed to have left the bar shortly after the incident in the direction of Stonegate.

PC Hayley Rowbotham of York police said: "The bar was very busy at the time and I am appealing to anyone who was in Kennedy's at around 9pm to 9.30pm on Sunday night to contact us, along with anyone who can help identify the suspect.

"A man who was enjoying a night out with friends has sustained serious injuries to his face which require long term treatment. It is vital that the person responsible is brought to justice."

Anyone who can help the investigation is asked to contact North Yorkshire Police on 101m select option 2 and ask for PC Hayley Rowbotham or email Hayley.rowbotham@northyorkshire.pnn.police.uk

If you wish to remain anonymous, you can pass information to Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111.

Please quote reference 12150055418 when passing on information.
