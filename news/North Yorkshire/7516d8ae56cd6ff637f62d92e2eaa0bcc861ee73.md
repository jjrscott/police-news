A Selby man was arrested in the early hours of Thursday 21 September 2017, on suspicion of breaking into a shed.

Police were contacted at 6.22am by a member of the public reporting a man gaining entry to one of the sheds at the Heworth allotments on Hempland Lane, York.

The witness stated the man had then been sighted filling a wheelbarrow with property from one of the allotments. He was then seen to wheel the wheelbarrow down Hempland Lane and head in the direction of Burnholme Drive and Bad Bargain Lane.

Having taken a description of the man, officers arrived at the scene at 6.30am and located the suspect pushing a wheelbarrow down the road which contained drills, watering cans, a leather toolbelt containing screwdrivers and other tools and a can of oil.

A 31-year-old man from Selby was arrested on suspicion of burglary with intent to steal and currently remains in police custody.
