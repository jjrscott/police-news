North Yorkshire Police is appealing for witnesses and information about a burglary that occurred in Scarborough.

It happened on North Marine Road, between Monday 27 and RTuesday 28 November 2017, whereby suspects entered the victims flat and stole two paintings and a wooden tribal mask.

The paintings are described as Russell Flint paintings. The first painting is A3 in size and features a desert scene with three people sat down with camels in the background. One of the people on the painting is a female with her breast exposed.

The second painting, also A3 in size; is a desert scene with a single topless female figure in foreground washing herself from a bowl. In the background there are camels.

The tribal mask is described as dark brown and unpainted, with two eyeholes and a mouth.

In particular, we are appealing for information from anyone who may have been offered these items.
