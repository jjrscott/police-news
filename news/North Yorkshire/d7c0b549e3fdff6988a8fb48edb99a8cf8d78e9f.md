Last modified: 17 October 2018 at 04:33pm

North Yorkshire Police are appealing for the owner of a bike that was stolen from the bike racks on St Andrewgate, York to come forward.

It happened on the evening of Thursday 17 August 2017 when a bicycle was captured on CCTV being stolen from St Andrewgate York. City of York CCTV camera operators quickly alerted police and a man was persued and arrested.

Two bicycles have been recovered as a result of the incident. One is a ladies bicycle and the other is a blue coloured mountain bike.

A 32-year-old man has been charged with three offences of bike theft and is due to appear in York Magistrate's court tomorrow.

If you have been the victim of bike theft please contact police with as much information as you can give including the time and place you last saw your bike, how it was secured, a full description of it including any stickers, serial number, colours, make, and any bags attached to it.

Anyone with information that could assist the investigation should contact us on 101, select option 2, and ask for Jill Barnard. You can also email Jill.Barnard@northyorkshire.pnn.police.uk

Please quote the North Yorkshire Police reference number 12170144593
