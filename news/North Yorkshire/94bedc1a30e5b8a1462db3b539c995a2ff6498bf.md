North Yorkshire Police is appealing for witnesses and information regarding a theft occurred in Starbeck, Harrogate.

The theft occurred at the junction of Forest Avenue and Knaresborough Road at approximately 11.30am on Sunday, 24 June.

It is believed a man approached an 11-year-old girl as she left the Co-operative store with her friend. Whilst she was carrying the change the man has then taken a Â£10 note from her hand. He has then made his way towards the Starbeck Railway Station.

The suspect is described as a white man, aged 50 to 60 years old of fat build. He was wearing a hat, jeans and a blue/green jacket at the time of the offence.

Anyone who had witnessed the incident or the events surrounding it is urged to contact North Yorkshire Police on 101, select option 2, and ask for PC Brendon Firth. You can also email Brendon.Firth@northyorkshire.pnn.police.uk

If you wish to provide information anonymously then the independent charity Crimestoppers can be contacted on 0800 555 111.

Please quote reference 12180112740 when providing information.
