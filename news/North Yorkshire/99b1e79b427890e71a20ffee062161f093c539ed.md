Last modified: 16 October 2018 at 11:14am

North Yorkshire Police is appealing for witnesses and information about two burglaries that occurred in the villages of Weeton and Huby between Harrogate and Leeds that may be linked.

They happened during the day while the owners were out on Wednesday 15 November 2017.

Two properties were entered by the offenders who went on to search the homes and steal items of jewellery.

It is believed that residents who live within the villages may have seen unfamiliar vehicles or people and this may assist police with any information they have.

We are conducting enquiries and are requesting the publicâ€™s assistance and in particular, we are appealing for information from anyone within the villages of Huby and Weeton who may have seen any person or vehicle acting in a suspicious way during the times of the offences or the days leading up to the offences.

Anyone with information that could assist the investigation should contact us on 101, select option 2, and ask for PC 342 Neil Sisson. You can also email neil.sisson@northyorkshire.pnn.police.uk

If you wish to remain anonymous, you can pass information to Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111.

Please quote the North Yorkshire Police reference number 12170205557.
