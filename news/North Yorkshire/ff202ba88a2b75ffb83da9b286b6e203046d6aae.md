Last modified: 16 March 2017 at 03:49pm

Police are appealing for any witnesses to a collision between a pedal cycle and a small red car, which occurred on Poppleton Road in York on Tuesday 7 March 2017 at around 7.15pm.

A 22-year-old man was cycling on his pedal cycle on Poppleton Road, heading uphill in the direction of Boroughbridge Road, when a small red, four-door car pulled out of a junction on the left hand side and collided with him, knocking him off his bike. The cyclist sustained minor injuries to his ankle and damage to his bike.

The driver, who initially stopped to help the cyclist but did not leave any details of his identity, is described as in his 20's, around 5'6" in height, of stocky build with short ginger hair. Its thought he had freckles and a red mark on his face, possibly a small cut.

His passenger was described as mid 20's with short brown hair, over 6ft tall and has an athletic build. He was wearing jeans and a zipped top.

Any witnesses or anyone who recognises the descriptions of the car or the men is asked to contact police on 101, select option two and speak to PC18 Carol Thompson. Alternatively you can email Carol.Thompson@northyorkshire.pnn.police.uk. Please quote reference 12170024754.

Alternatively you can contact Crimestoppers anonymously on 0800 555 111.
