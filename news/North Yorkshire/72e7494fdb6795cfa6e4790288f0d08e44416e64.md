Last modified: 18 June 2018 at 11:16am

Police are appealing for witnesses following a serious two-vehicle collision at Staxton near Scarborough.

The collision occurred at about 6.10pm on Saturday 16 June 2018, on the B1249 near the top of Staxton Hill, at the junction with the entrance to RAF Staxton Wold.

It involved a black Vauxhall Mokka and a yellow Peugeot 107. The occupants of the Vauxhall, a man in his 30s and a young girl from Humberside, were taken to Scarborough Hospital with minor injuries. The driver of the Peugeot, a woman in her 30s from Scarborough, was taken to Hull Royal Infirmary with serious but non-life threatening injuries.

The road was closed until 11.10pm to allow for collision investigation and recovery of vehicles.

Anyone who witnessed the collision, or saw either vehicle prior to it, is asked to contact TC Mark Patterson of North Yorkshire Police's Roads Policing Group, by emailing Mark.Patterson@northyorkshire.pnn.police.uk or phoning 101. Please quote reference number 12180107496.
