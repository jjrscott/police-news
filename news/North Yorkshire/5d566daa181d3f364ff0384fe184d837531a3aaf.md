Detectives have charged two men from Liverpool with possession of heroin and cocaine with intent to supply as they continue the fight against drugs in Scarborough.

Officers arrested the two men aged 18 and 24, along with a 30-year-old Scarborough man, when they raided a property on Gildercliffe on Saturday 10 January 2015.

During a search of the property police found heroin and cocaine worth £7,000 and around £600 in cash.

The 24-year-old man from Liverpool and the 30-year-old man from Scarborough were both charged with two counts of possession with intent to supply Class A drugs and two counts of being concerned in the supply of Class A drugs.

The 18-year-old man from Liverpool was charged with two counts of possession with intent to supply Class A drugs.

All three men were remanded in custody and are due to appear at Scarborough Magistrates' Court on Monday 12 January 2015.
