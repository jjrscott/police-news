Officers are appealing for two cyclists to come forward following an incident in Hemingbrough.

Officers are appealing for two cyclists to come forward following an incident in Hemingbrough.

The incident occurred at around 6.20pm on Friday, 13 July when the cyclists and a woman in her 40s were travelling along Main Street.

The woman, who was driving a silver car, was involved in an altercation with a male cyclist before damage was caused to her vehicle. The other male cyclist was not involved in any offence.

The cyclists are both described a male and aged in their 50s. One of the men was wearing a red top with black shorts and the other wearing a grey cycling top and black shorts.

Officers are currently investigating the circumstances surrounding the incident and are asking for anyone who can assist them to come forward.

Information can be passed to North Yorkshire Police on 101, select option 2, and ask for Nigel Collins. You can also email nigel.collins@northyorkshire.pnn.police.uk

If you wish to provide information anonymously then the independent charity Crimestoppers can be contacted on 0800 555 111.

Please quote the North Yorkshire Police reference number of 12180127152.
